Viking

2013-01-23 12:07:00 UTC

The timers are good for a ref and improving but they're usually a bit faster than real life.

I've ran Race chrono, Trackmaster, both with external gps and now a Starlane gps timer and they all read faster than the official transponder. The starlane is closest at about 0.2-0.5 sec but the remote gps phone ones could be 1-2sec a lap out as sometimes it will glitch and miss a bit of the track out so when you look at it the line goes right across the grass on the apex.

They are good though and the best way to use them would be to try and string consistent laps with them and remove the rogue ones. Like for example if your doing 1.50-1.51's and you get a single 1.47, then disregard it. Realistically unless you do something dramatically different your not going to jump 3 sec a lap. Only work with the times that are consistant and take them with a pinch of salt to be maybe a second out.

It interesting that 1 sec a lap real time is alot but over a lap its not much. It's 2ft later on the brakes, 1ft closer to apex, 0.5 sec faster on the throttle. Small things but add up over a lap. Following guys who are a little faster will show that. You'll be like "theyre not pulling much, only a few feet every corner", but then you get 3/4 round the lap and they're 20ft ahead and thats your second.

hjp1968

2013-01-23 12:09:00 UTC

You can use an external GPS Bluetoothed to your phone, if you get a 5Hz (5 updates a second) one the accuracy improves a lot, 10hz (10 updates a second) ones can actually give too much info and swamp the phone buggering up the accuracy.
