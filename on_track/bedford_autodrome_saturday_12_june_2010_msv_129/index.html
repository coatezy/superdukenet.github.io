It took the following sessions up to lunch time to learn where i was going (and where i was going wrong) and apart from Bedfords annoying noise policy and the marshalls insisting we stop grouping together on track to cut the noise , its about as relaxed as a trackday gets! small groups, wide track, good vision, TONS of run off and seemed like plenty of grip - happy dayz

Jody must get the prize for `biggest balls` of the day for his near highside which i witnessed from the pit lane, although the way he landed back on the bike i expect his balls are now flat

2010-06-13 18:08:00 UTC

Great day, marred only by being totally knackered from the off through lack of sleep and overwork the past two weeks. Concentration and energy were just not up to the level you need to ride at track speeds, there was nothing I could do. In the early afternoon I fell asleep sitting in the pits and it was only when the fast group Superdukes pulled up right next to me that I woke up from my dreams.

Nice to meet Big Ash and Twisted Jester, Jody and the others.

But great track, I will be back for another go. Strange to have no elevations 'cos of the flat landscape and this means that it's hard at first to learn the course because it all looks the same. Maybe there won't be another chance because they had a LOT of issues with noise and one guy from MSV was telling me that this was their last chance to prove to the council that bike noise was something they could keep under control. (which they clearly couldn't as this relied on bikes never getting together in groups of more than TWO!!).
