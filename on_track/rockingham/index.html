But isn't it the case that unless you're running track specific rubber they're not really needed as road rubber is perfectly capable of dealing with numerous heat cycles whereas track rubber needs to be kept hot between sessions so as to prolong the life of the tyre.

At least that's what Luke said last time tyre warmers were mentioned

But what he wrote did make perfect sense alongside what information I already had.

For the sole purpose of them just being hot from the first lap I don't think it's really worth the hassle of needing a generator or power outlet and losing time between sessions sorting it all out as they're only going to stay as hot as you can ride them and conditions allow anyway once you're on track, so if you're on road rubber then to my mind it's less bother to just give it a couple of laps to warm them up as well as a chance to get your head in gear too.

Obviously with track rubber it's a must have.
