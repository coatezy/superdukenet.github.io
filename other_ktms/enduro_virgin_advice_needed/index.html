Ah I wise move Luke and one on you may or may not live to regret

Personally I'd def go for electric start. Kick start is ok if you are shit hot and never stall or are a hard northerner like Sabre. I'm neither so it was top of the list of things required.

I'd stay away from strokers as the power is in a nutty place and its torque you need to get yourself out of the shit. Plus the lack of engine braking is a problem on descents. Oh and they fooking stink

Kit wise you'll need some good boots. Alpinestars are about £150 and are great. Sidi Crossfires are really nice but at £240-ish are a lot of dosh. You will need body armour. Best is to get the stuff built into a vest. Knox do some nice stuff (£100). Needs to cover elbows, shoulders, back and chest. Maybe shorts with hips protection as well and def half decent knee armour is a must (£50). This sounds like a lot of protection but trust me you will be crashing. Helmets are not badly priced compared to road ones. I bought a No Fear on offer for £50 down from a one'r. Does the job nicely. Then all you need is clothing and there is a wide range of that. Guess you are looking at about £150-200 for trousers, top and jacket. Lastly you will need a bag on the bike for spare inner tubes, tyre irons and tool kit.

Bike wise the 400exc is a good first time bike I've been led to believe. Has plenty of power but delivers it in a linear way unlike the 450 which can be brutal and want to rip your arms from their sockets given the opportunity From 2008 KTM bought out a 250 exc-f 4 stroke, not sure what that is like. If you have say £3,5-4k to spend you can find yourself a really nice bike. No point in a dealer purchase as the basically isn't a warranty on dirt bikes anyway. Just make sure it has had the oil dropped regularly I'd be tempted to check the magnetic sump bolt before handing any money over. I can't comment on any other makes. Sabre sear by the XR 400 but its kick start.

Can't think of anything else. Look forward to seeing you at Enduroland for some crash action
