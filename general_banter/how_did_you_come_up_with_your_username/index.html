It's all tied into my tattoo.
I'm Mel, and I bear the Aegis.

Greek mythology has it that Athena, Goddess of wisdom and teaching, borrowed her father Zeus' shield, or Aegis, at times of battle.

She also had strong connections with Medusa, who claimed to be more attractive and intelligent than Athena.
Athena turned her hair into snakes, and it was the contrast between the aesthetic face and hideous snakes that petrified folk.
Medusa then claimed to still be more attractive, so Athena sent Perseus to kill her, which he duly did.

To show the world what happened to those that upset her, Athena nailed Medusa's head to the Aegis.

I therefore have the symbol of the Goddess of Wisdom and Teaching on my back, so she is looking out for me and I can get on with my job with her favour.

The owl in top of the Aegis is an Athena Noctua, or little owl, the Owl of wisdom.
Again, tied into the Athena myth, especially connected with her City, Athens.
