bic_bicknell

2013-02-18 06:16:00 UTC

If I ever had to do a lot of work on the engine I'd consider getting the casings made black - it's been a mission over the years to get rid of the silver and other finishes. I've got a subframe and engine hangers to be powder-coated when I get the time.

I used to have round Akros with carbon sleeves but compromised on the Evo's because they came with larger bore Titanium pipework. If I could think of a way to make the sleeves out of carbon I would but they are a bummer of a cross-section to try and fabricate. I was thinking of waiting for when I need to repack these Evo silencers and when I have the sleeve apart to take a casting of the internal shape to use as a male mould to form carbon weave sheet around. Then make a pair of sleeves over this. Having done a fair bit of casting and fabrication in the past my only concern with this is how to get the sleeve off the male mould - because it will not have a taper and so the carbon sleeve will not slide off easily. The only way I've so far thought about a solution to this is to make a male mould out of sections so that it comes apart in bits. In plastic injection moulding this is called a collapsable core and will let you get the inside part of the tool out of the moulded component even when you have an undercut.

Anyway, a project for the future when I have more time, ie. when my terrible-two twins are grown up and left home, in about 16 years!!!
