Its a quote that is as old as English literature.
I believe the original is in Chaucer (c 1390):
" For thogh we slepe or wake, or rome, or ryde, Ay fleeth the tyme; it will no man abyde."

it is repeated in diaries, books and documents changing over the years.
For example we have
Tyde nor time tarrieth no man.
1592 R. Greene

and
Time and tide tary on no man.
1639 J. Clarke


Personally I've always wanted a big W tatooed on each bum cheek so when I bend over it spells WOW!
Although seriously I like the idea of having the Chaucer quote in old english script around the left bicep and
"Therefore, send not to know for whom the bell tolls, it tolls for thee" John Donne around the right
