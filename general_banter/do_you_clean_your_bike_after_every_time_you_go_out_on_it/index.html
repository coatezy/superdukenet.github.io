2014-03-01 22:22:00 UTC

I cheat.

I live in a warm, and very dry climate. Even though it's raining today, it's plenty warm enough to ride. We simply don't have those cold winter months where the bike sits idle.

That's not to say that there aren't consequences for not cleaning. As it's also a marine environment (I am near the ocean), and extremely dusty. A combination which if ignored, can result in serious corrosion issues. Too common on used bikes in the area ...

While I don't break out the hose after every ride (the SD is my daily commuter after all), I do tend to look after things, and clean particularly thoroughly whenever I perform a service (which on the SD is far too frequent); e.g. Oil and tyre changes, chain cleanings, or when doing a modification or unscheduled repair. I concentrate mostly on the areas of the bike, and those near, wherever the service takes place.

One upside of this approach, is that it forces me to examine the bike closely as I go, greatly increasing the likelihood that I'll notice something if in need of attention.

Otherwise, I do the occasional wash - the wheels and radiator being the most time consuming parts to clean and get dry (even with blowers, compressed air, and high pressure nitrogen available). Thorough removal of rinse (tap) water (drying) is important here, as we have high calcium content, which creates other problems.

Like you, I resent the tedium of (and time required for) cleaning bikes, but the results usually justify the effort at least. It seems like time I could be doing something more important (to me).
