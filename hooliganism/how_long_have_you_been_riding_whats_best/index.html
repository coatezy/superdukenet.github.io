I loved reading this thread.Brought back so many memories.
At 55 I was very lucky growing up in southern Calif.I started on dirt bikes Hodaka ace 90 at 12. Then the fun starts 250 husky,400 CZ
Then the jap bikes took over.
I had them all 250 2 strokes Loved my Yz's raced them for years.
In the 80's I got into street bikes.Started with a 83 Honda Nighthawk 650.Great bike My first new street bike.Put the best tyres on and a kerker pipe and went blasting thru the canyons.

This was the golden age there was no such thing as a sport bike.I went to the GP's and saw the greats the king was amazing.Randy Mamola
crazy guy doing stopies before anyone knew what to call them.I got to see this young kid ride his Honda into history winning the 250 and the 500 title.You old guys will know who I am talking about.Man was he fast.
After that the sport bike war hit GPZ's VFR's I saw the first ones and had one thought I was Kenny on this bike.

Then here comes this thing called a gsxr wow it was light years ahead.Now you could have a real race bike.For us it was the real deal.
I did superbike school with Kieth Code and I was hooked. Tracks when ever I could Willow Springs and Laguna.
Thinks about it I was riding on the same race track as Kenny Roberts and the best in the world.
I did club racing for about five years.I was in the M/C business as well.Motorcycles were my life.I still raced mx too.

I had a couple of kids and had to make a choice.MX won.Got the kids bikes and we all had fun.

I gave up street riding just was not fun in L A I could not get the same rush I got from the track.
At 48 I got a Harley I lived in Az it was safer.Plus my back could not take a sportbike anymore
I started seeing the super moto's and dreamed of them.Wanted a duke since day one.
Just did not want to spend the 16K.
I was missing some thing The Harley was ok but it was not enough.
After a bad back for many years I got a surgery and now I was ok! no more pain.
Got a aprilia DD750 loved that bike.As we all know aprilia has a bad dealer network.
I sold it. Then out of nowwhere I got this deal on a 08 duke 200 miles with 15 months of warranty.
I had to have it 7900 bucks the bike of my dreams finally.

Full circle I have got all the go fast mods joined trackdayriders and plan to get back out on the track. This year.
Life is strange I never thought I would do this again.Now I can't wait.I still ride MX.
My duke is my bike of choice now.Hope I can see and meet some of you guys at the track.
Life is good at 55 I got new parts in my back. Good as new.

Hi All,

This thread would make a good starting point for a psychology thesis on why old people people do crazy things ( and in their youth ... it must be genetic ).

The thing that stands out most here is that a lot of the elder ( and still not sensible ) SD owners have had a past filled with socially unacceptable but outrageously capable two strokes.
It really does say something about you lot. Why ? Well, in the day they had the biggest bang for the buck and were usually the underdog in a world of bigger capacity four strokes. They sounded nasty with some proper pipes and were smelly and oily. The general public didn't like them and most traditional bikers didn't like them either. So you were obviously antisocial misfits and probably a cheap to. I traded my Norton fastback that I'd owned and loved for 8 years for a new RG500 Gamma in 1985 and all my twin riding mates thought I'd gone mad ( wish I still had both now ).

So what's different today, not much by the stories in this thread. The SD still has distinctive styling that a lot of people don't get and underdog status among the latest rockets. It has a throttle response that's not quite a two stroke power band but is still pretty snappy and has genuine sporting abilities and is a whole lot of fun. Which is just what we were looking for back then.

KTM seem to build their bikes with a bit more attitude than most of the others. Which I guess, is why they appeal to you crazy lot
