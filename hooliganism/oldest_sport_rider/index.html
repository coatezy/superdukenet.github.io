This was in 1950 after I chopped off the handlebars, fenders ( that is a model A spare tyre cover on the front fender. It later got put on the rear.My older sister on the back ,the last time she ever rode on my motorcycles.LOL Check the oil spots on the ground.Bought this bike front old Ed Kretz for twenty bucks out of his boneyard behind his shop in Monery Park Ca. in 49" Ed was the first winner of the Daytona 200 in 38" his # was 38 also. I raced this bike with his son Ed Ketz Jr. at the old Ascot speedway short track when it was in Lincoln Park L.A. before it was moved to Gardena,Ca. in 53' ( needless to say I couldnt win, all the other guys had british Iron at that time and they would just eat me up.Another story about that racetrack ; Before I was old enough to race I lived near the track at that time and when they werent racing during the week my friends and I who had bikes ( a mustang and another Indian ) we knew where the loose boards in the fence were so we used to sneak in and race around the track for practice then when I was 15 I lied and told them I was 16 so I could race. I was pretty good but I still could not win.It was so much fun though, until we were doing a TT and someone T boned me and broke my shoulder.but that is another story............Later
