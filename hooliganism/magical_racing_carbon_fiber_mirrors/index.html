SDNerd

2012-03-28 16:48:00 UTC

Nice ! Now you can see the whole road in front of you. Just don't forget about the late bitch on your six!
A small press can work but you have to remove the lines and re-bleed. A small machinest vice could press the pin out without removing the clamp from the lines.
This is how I would / will remove the roll pin. Get the largest drift punch that will pass thru the hole and a small hammer. Move the clamp as far as you can to the right but still on the straight section of the bars. Now make a backing to hold the clamp inplace as you tap the pin thru. Use a flat bar, 1/8 inch thich to rest against the plastic switch housing to spread the load. Place a small socket between the flat bar and the clamp to drive the pin thru. Snug the clamp / socket / flat bar / placstic switch housing all together. Snug the fastener just over finger tight. The roll pin should not take a lot of force to remove.

Replacing the pin .....MIght be a combo of a small "C" clamp or the trusty hammer and larger drift punch with the flat bar socket combo you used to remove the pin. If you are using the FORD or DRIBBLE tool (hammer), wrap your handle bars with some rubber cushion of some sort to avoid pecker marks

Well Indy, I think you now have the best mirrors on the market money can get. Frickin' ART....Enjoy...others will too. You will be hearing alot of "WOW"S"

Got a story about a "C" clamp, a Yamaee TT500 and a bad top end. I bought this bike w/ 500 miles on it. The PO installed a larger carb and free flow exuaust. It never ran right. If you got it started it would smoke so bad you could not see thru the smoke. I got pissed at it and tore the head off. I wanted to see the valve guides and the bore. Not having the correct valve spring removal tool, I MIckey Moused a large "C" clamp and a hand wrench to compress the valve spring to remove the valve keeper. Shit happened quick and the keeper shot out and hit my top front tooth...HARD....Oh fook that stung. I thought for sure I broke my tooth. I cracked it, a hair line. I went to the doc and that fooker tried his damnest to break it out. WTF are you doing??? It healed back it's self.
Moral of this story is eye protection and use the correct tool for the job and look out with the "C" clamp ! If the keeper would have hit my eye it might have put it out.
Oh! My fix on the bike was a over bore and new piston and valve guides. The shop reshaped the carb slide with a rat tail file to match a bike with the same mods. Lots of TT 500's used for flat track / TT bikes. The origional shape made it run like it was running on choke all the time. Ran great after that.
