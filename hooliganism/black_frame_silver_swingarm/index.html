Morning all, I've recently upgraded from an '01 SV650 to an '07 SD 990 and while it's an awesome machine and in excellent shape, there's a few things about it that seem slightly odd....

For a start it has 2 keys, one for the ignition, one for the tank and seat. Next up, it has 'R' fairings when it's a non-R, but also the colour scheme is not one I can find elsewhere, with black and white panels, a black frame and a silver swingarm.

It's in such great condition I'd be really surprised if it had been significantly rebuilt, so I'm wondering how much of it could have left the factory as-is or if there's something fishy happening.
There's no sign it's ever fallen over, the mileage is low and service history correct...

My guess would be you've got a late registered 06 bike, hence the non radial front brake calipers and the silver swing arm.

Someone has gone to the trouble to upgrade the headlight and maybe also the dash, fuel tank & shrouds and then either changed the seat or the ignition.
I think it was a common upgrade to make the earlier versions look more like the later ones but also to use the 07 onwards larger fuel tank.
