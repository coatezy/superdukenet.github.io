that quoted weight figure for the ducati will be dry, i promise.

That in turn will make it heavier or on par with the duke when fuelled.

We are comparing dry weight to dry weight. KTM lists the Super Duke at 186kg dry. That's 41 pounds heavier than the Ducati. you have to add oil, coolant and fuel to both bikes, so while they will both gain weight, the Ducati will remain about 40 pounds lighter than the KTM, and that is a huge difference.

On the engine power advantage......what good did it do for the monster s4rs?? A bike with fantastic specs, componentry etc......just unfortunately fell short underneath a spirited rider. Fantastic for posers and straight line heroes.

The Monster is a 15 year old design. It has been updated a few times, but it is still from the early 1990's. The Streetfighter is based off of the 1098 platform. Again, big difference!

I would wager that with a control rider (a sport orientated one at that), for comparitive purposes, an sd would be quicker over a given section of twisties.

I guess time will show whether or not you are correct. However, I expect that the comparison tests will include things other than the tightest mountain roads. If all I rode on was the Tail of the Dragon, and I was Rossi, perhaps the KTM will prove to be the "better" ride.

But by and large, as a street bike, and one that might see some track use, I am putting my money on the Ducati.

Ducati's, by and large, are owned and ridden by those more concerned with image than performance. And in road legal trim, like for like they dont perform as well as their direct competitors......that goes double for their 1098 model. and no.......the 20k R does not have a direct rival.

A Ford is probably easier to drive then a Ferrari, I don't know that it makes the Ford a better car.

If all we cared about was the best performance, we would all buy a 2008 Fireblade and call it a day. But Ducati and KTM owners alike do care about image, and they do want something difference from the "unwashed masses".

Believe it or not i dont hate ducati. And i dont own a superduke.

Perhaps if you did own and ride both you would be better prepared to discuss the differences?

Experience however, has shown that on the road and in road legal trim at least, ducati's have tended to look better than they go.......

I don't have the same experience. It all comes down to the rider and I have seen some wicked fast Ducati riders up in the mountains. I do think that they may be more cautious and responsible and less "squidly", as they tend to be more expensive and all that.
