Replacing the airbox with either the motohooligan or the CPR system makes all the difference, this together with the other must do's really.
Start with dumping the anchor called exhaust, it weights a ton, gets stinking hot and robs power.
A proper map, SAS and O2 sensors switched off also makes a difference to make it run better and to get rid of the dreaded surge.

I have the Mivv Suono's in SS. You have to like them to live with them. The finish is also not as god as some, I had to "work" the brackets a bit to get a good fit.
The original db killers are very restrictive = useless
Without it is very loud! I made some perforated inserts myself so it is in the middle. Sure I wont pass any trackday noise test in the UK though.
Bottom is original, top is the home made.

Top 5 mods...

Let's try engine wise:

- akrapovic ( less weight, less heat, more noise but not over the top )
- tuneecu ( cheap cable, several maps available from lot's of nice guys around here )
- remove SAS, switch of O2 sensor

These will give a better low rpm drivability ( higher gear, lower rpm without the shaking a rattle of the original set up

- motobox or other filter box (
- rc8 trumpets
- oval admissions butterflies
- change ecu map setup
- start again

And some horse power is released

Chassis set up:

- WP or Scott steering damper ( for the lightweight front end do less shakes and tank slappers lol )
- new rearsets to get the foot pegs higher
- depending on the weight: new front end springs or revalve
- suspension and sag setup
-smaller and lighter batery
- carbon dymags wheels

Touring:

- tank bag
- rear support for side bags
- lot's of other make do bag inventions ( not the best bike for touring options )

Bling:

5 is not enough lol

- carbon or fiberglass rear cowl
- carbon front and rear mudguard
- carbon lower spoiler
- carbon radiator covers
- orange anodised:
frame plugs,
bar weights replacement
clutch, brake reservoir covers
- small and adjustable levers
- smaller licence and side markers support
- etc etc etc

A: Tuneecu
B: Tuneecu...
You know where I'm going with this. Tuneecu is you r best investment. It allows you to remove the SAS, O2 sensors, remove the restrictor in 2nd-3rd gear, upload a decent map AND your diagnostics + throttle body sync.... waow all for the price of the cable and a big thanks to Tom and Alain.
To get results get rid of the exhaust boat anchor and get a proper air filter. A custom map is recommended but you might find something suitable for your bike in the list here on the forum.
Set your suspension up properly, no need to trow money at this till you are getting at the limit of it.
The rest is optional and or cosmetic as you like or can afford...

Probably forgotten a few things but I guess that is covered by other members...
