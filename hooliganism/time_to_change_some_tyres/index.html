Time to change some tyres

thewall67

2009-03-04 03:03:00 UTC

So I had three bikes that needed new rubber and the thought of taking the tyres off to lug them to the closest trust worthy shop (28mi/40minutes), pay $35usd per tyre ($40 if I don't buy my tyres from them which means my tyres would cost me about $20 more), drive home, then drive back to get them another day and have a 75% chance they will be nicked up didn't thrill me. This fun event prompted me to break open the piggy bank on a new tyre changing system.

It's a No-Mar tyre changer, $595usd (free shipping, nice incentive as it weights 80lbs). There is always a guy who can put his tyres on a 5gal bucket and bust tyres and only nick the rims "a little" with only 35 bucks invested in the system. That ain't for me. I would 1. make the rim look like total crap that way or 2. be in the garage for 8hrs trying not to make them look like total crap and make them look just sort of crappy. The first step is knowing your limits.

Anyway, this thing is well known and proven to work. Speaking now from experience I can tell you it does. I tried a couple times to scratch the hell out of my rims but nothing. Not so much as a pin head size nick. Plus, with three changes it will about 40% pay for itself. Plus again, I like working on my own stuff!

The biggest problem is it's to cold to change tyres in my garage. Solution...bring the garage into the house. I have a spare room I'm not using for more than storage so I setup the changer in there. I made a 4 x 4ft 3/4inch plywood base mounted on two 2 x 4inch supports. The changer is mounted with four 2.5inch bolts with galvanized steel supports. After changing two sets of tyres I have no regrets on the outlay of cash. Works great!
