Ticking Sound...

boney95

2011-04-27 11:46:00 UTC

Hey guys, I get this consistant "tick tick tick" sound coming out of my exhaust. You can hear it at idle, and can really hear it at overrun, and WOT. You can't hear it if you're on the bike, only if you're standing on the street. The sound seems to be originating somewhere near the rear of the engine, but it's hard to tell.

I might have caused this problem myself. Last summer when I got my FMF slip-ons they didn't fit properly. So to make them fit, my dumb ass banged the shit out of the left can (with only my hand) to get the left cans pipe off of the subframe. I actually had to sand down a little bit of the subframe to help with this. Now since then I returned the cans to FMF for repair. They sent the same cans back to me, now fitting like a glove, while replacing both of the cores and searched to see if the "ticking" was coming from them. The ticking is NOT coming from the cans. So, I'm thinking I messed something up with the headers somewhere?

Any thoughts on this, and what I could have jacked up by banging the shit out of that left can (while fully mounted)? The bike itself runs great with no problems, so I'm assuming the "ticks" are not coming from inside the engine.

I still have the stock exhaust on my 08SD but I do have a constant ticking sound after I park it after a sprited ride. I believe my tick is due to heat expansion / contraction. I did have a 86 600 Ninja that had a loose baffle inside the muffler that rattled when I hit 6-7k rpm. It was a dead giveaway that I was about to get with the program to my riding buds. Try to loosen all your fasteners to the exhaust and retighten them, and keep you eyes open for the unexpected. If you take the mufflers all the off, shake them for loose parts.
