Sold the SDR - and signing out... (ish)

Dutch

2012-03-05 13:39:00 UTC

Hi all,

It's been a while since I was a daily contributor here (so some of you are thinking, who the F is Dutch?), ...but after deliberating for almost a year I've finally taken the plunge and actually sold my beloved SuperDuke R, (...to a lovely fella called Nick from Haywards Heath).

It was my sixth or seventh KTM, and my third bike with an LC8 engine, so not owing a bright-orange Austrian V-Twin is gonna be very odd, but in truth my tastes and needs were changing. As I get older I'm losing the head-down, elbows-up aggression that I used to have to conjour-up to do the SDR justice on the roads. Riding with less conviction than 110% on an SDR is like asking Angelina Jolie for a hand shandy when she's offering toad in the hole... To underline my aging outlook ("no, son, let's WALK down the hill and f*ck ALL them cows") my second girly-bike - a Ducati Sport Classic 1000 (see below) became my favoured daily ride, just cos it was just easier to have fun on, even on a track day. Weird but true.

Over the last two weeks I rode the Superduke more than ever, knowing it was gonna leave me soon, and I fell in love with the beast all over again... but not enough to keep her standing there in my back yard, expensively insured and depreciating fast, while I pansy-about on a cafe racer. She deserves a better life in more appreciative hands.

The Superduke was, and is, one of the greatest bikes ever made, and those who ride them are an opinionated, maverick bunch of wankers, whom I have loved to know and ride with. The new version with 1200cc won't be as good as the original, cos KTM broke the mold on that one, and I will look back with pride at having once owned such a notorious, ground-breaking, class-defining, machine, and riding with such complete lunatics with two fingers up to polite riding, and gathering a host of biking stories to keep me in free beers for many years to come.

AGRO, Dribble, Cheers fellas.

Klincky - def up for a beer, and want to see how your SD comes out.

Bic - The new owner, Nick, is a sound guy. Ex RC8 and Fireblade owner (saw the light at last).

Nampus, the list of mods for the SC range is even bigger than for the SDR - so look out, she'll get prettier and prettier - and quicker too. 100bhp at the rear wheel on a 175kg bike, with top spec suspension, that looks like it was made in 1972, is where I wanna be this summer.

Samba - Hmmm. If you fancy a supermoto, I'd look at a Husky 510SMR. Awesome bike, and as an ex LC4 and SMC owner, I'm not ashamed to admit that the Husky is the better bike. In fact I might get one myself. It'd give the SD a run for it's money in the twisties. The LC4 is a tractor by comparison.

fatbob - I hope I'm wrong too, but IMO what KTM should've done to the SD is simply lost 25kg, lowered and shortened the whole thing by 10%, upgraded the suspension further, added a sliper clutch as strandard, and sorted the fuelling. Job done.

...The last thing the SDR needs is more cc (on a naked bike ) and electronic rider aids, so that idiots can ride them as well as more experienced riders. I liked being in a exclusive club of riders who had a level of skill that allowed them to ride such a peice of kit. Anyone can ride a S1000RR or Panigale these days. ...Boring.

I think KTM Also need to move on with thier design department. A few curves wouldn't go amiss. The first SD was the prettiest - look at the lovely curved tank.
