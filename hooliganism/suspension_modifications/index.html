A lot of people on this site rave about the WP suspension on the SD. I'm not one of em. The standard components are just average run of the milll suspenders.

I've had my forks and shock revalved and sprung by Race-Tech and noticed an imediate improvement over stock. I've been playing with the damping ove the last couple years and am getting it to where it really works well...the fork especially. I've got it to the point where feels really planted with lots of feedback approaching the limit. The shock is a different matter. Due to design limitaions (in my opinion) you can get it working on small stuff (little bumps/ripples) or big stuff (freeway expansion joints) but not both. Always seems to be a compromise. I believe this is due to the lack of a linkage which provides a progressive rate throught the shock stroke. The WP shock provides somewhat of a rising rate but not as well as a linkage assembly.

Anyway, you've just got to play with the various damping circuits until you reach a compromise you can live with. I'm still trying.

John
