I go with Joe, 2 fingers on the brake lever pretty much the whole time when on the road - there's loads of spazwits out there and they're all out to get you, covering the brake gives you a nanosecond headstart on them. However, when you apply the brake come off the throttle and start down shifting as you approach a junction. With practice this process will become smooth and controlled. You will come to a halt in 1st ready to pull away again, rather than having to prod about at the box trying to find the right gear.
Just remember to try to avoid using the throttle or the brake like an on/off switch, smoothness (and practice) is everything.

Ducati Pete

2009-01-18 09:46:00 UTC

Graham Hill should be my favorite bend on the Indy as I usually like left handers better than right handers. I have been a bit ambitious there a few times in the past and wound up on the paint on the right hand track edge on exit. Bit scary when it is wet. So it takes me a while to build up to it now.

I know a guy that is making a lot of time there by taking a real early entry line. I havent seen it but heard from a mutual friend. So next time we are both on track I am going to try to get behind him through there and see what he does.

I used to feel the same about riding in the wet. My road riding style of easy on/ off with the throttle helped me pass guys that were quicker than me in the dry. And this advantage at the track gave me more confidence in the tyres on the road.

Last year I tried race wets. The grip they give is amazing. You can get on the power as early in the dry, lean over as far as in the dry and if you hit standing water all you get is a zzzzzzzzzzzzzzzzzzzzzzzzzzp then the tyre is gripping again. Even melting snow didnt faze them at Snett last Nov. You end the day thinking you are Chris Vermuelen

It takes a few wet sessions to get full confidence in them but then you start hoping for wet trackdays so that you can use them again. Lucky we live in the UK so that isnt a problem

If I ever get spare wheels for SD I will take a set of wets to every trackday. The trouble is they make road tyres, 2CT's, Rain 2's etc feel like banana skins in the wet. So its goodbye to your extra confidence on the road.
