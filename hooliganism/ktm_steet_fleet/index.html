RED ZX

2011-01-29 09:53:00 UTC

Despite some wet roads I had a good time today.

First up was the 690 SMC, what a fun bike, would take a while to get used to. For wheelstands I like to loft it in first then short shift to 2nd and accelerate as I slowly let the front back down, when I tried this on the 690 it did not have the torque to carry on in 2nd. It would come up lightning fast, but you would need to carry the wheel much higher if you wanted any distance, something I was not comfortable with on a bike that isn't mine.

Next was a standard Superduke, things I noticed were I missed the extra 1000rpm, hit the limiter a few times, once while overtaking. Seat is much more comfortable and I could see myself riding one for hours on end. Engine much softer and smoother with stock mapping.

3rd up was the Duke 690, someone else took the R every time so I had to make do with the basic. Not a bike for me at all. Sure it was light and handled well, but the seat pushed me too far forward, I was more comfortable sitting on the pillion section. The little rises in the seat that are meant to keep you in place made it difficult for me to be where I wanted to be. It was gutless as all hell. Unless I was sitting right back on over the tail-light I could not get the front up nicely. Even on a wet slippery road I could not get any wheelspin, even when leant over.

Last bike I rode was the standard RC8. After everyone else, including the "experienced lead rider" telling me to watch the tyres and that the throttle is really jerky, I found it great. Those guys must be really ham-fisted or have a death grip when riding as I did not experience any throttle snatch at all. That engine is just great, and the chassis was not too track focussed which surprised me. The roads we took were pretty decent, fair few pot-holes but it was a local road to me so I knew it fairly well. Plenty of wind protection on the RC8, even up to 200 without tucking in. Brakes were good too. I want that engine in a SD with about 4 more teeth on the rear sprocket.

After all was said and done I was quite happy to jump back on my SDR, easily the best KTM to suit what I want. But I would like a SMC for rainy days and an RC8 for track work. That's not asking too much is it?

Hi mate,
Went to Maroochy today as well and rode the RC8R, the SMC and the regular 690 Duke...
The two first rides were quick as the roads had dried up and the lead dude was enjoying himself.. The RC8R was fun and powerful, with great brakes but the riding position is very committed and reminded me why I quit sportsbikes.. The SMC as said, was fun! No weight and awesome to ride motard style. I did miss the extra grunt though as the little 690 isn't very powerful at the speeds we were doing.. The 690 Duke as Red said was boring and gutless.. It was also a slower run which made it a bit less exciting. But best of all, getting back on my SD with Akras and Marbox and 16/41 gearing was just bliss! The perfect bike for the road! Sure, for the track the RC8 engine would be fun but for the road, this thing is sex on wheels! I love my SD!
