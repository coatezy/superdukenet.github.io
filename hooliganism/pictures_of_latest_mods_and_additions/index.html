Lowrance

2011-05-03 13:57:00 UTC

Yep, disks are Carrozzeria and tyres are Q2s (didn't know there was anything newer than these).

Been running Q2's for a while now (on my 4th set) and like them a lot. They wear really well (compared to other mfrs tyres) and stick n slide predictably. I get 1K miles outta every tyre I've tried on this bike with the fronts wearing out as quickly as the rears. For some reason I can get almost 2 rears to a front with these Q2s (around 1200 to the rear and 2000 miles on the front).

Did a track day a couple of months ago at Chuckwalla here in So Cal and was going to throw some D211s on the bike but couldn't locate any in time so I left on a fairly new set of Q2s that were on the bike. To say I was impressed would be an understatement. I was not going race pace but I certainly wasn't sitting around watching the grass grow either. The tyres warmed up quickly which I expected but what I didn't expect was that they stayed so consitent for the full 20 minute sessions. Was learning a new track so by second half of the day really started getting down to some faily quick times and tyres were still hanging nicely. Only one spot on the track (turn 9 comming onto back shute headed to fast esses) I was pushing hard enough to slide and it did so predictably and consistently.

Gotta say the SD surprsed me that day. Busted it's track cherry. Didn't think it'd be all that suitable for track days (more suited to tight canyon roads). Caught myself looking at all the nice track hardware and was starting to jones for a new bike. Got out on the track and those feeling were soon replaced with wonder as to why all of these nice bikes were in my way (I was in a pretty quick B group as well). Quite a few people were comming over to check out the strange bike that was blowing them into the weeds...that was fun!

So, in lieu of new bike decided to drop some coin on old faithful here and am glad I did. Looks good, goes like stink and is unique! What more could you ask for?

Sorry, little off topic....

Lowrance

2011-05-06 14:03:00 UTC

Had to build the 520 kit myself. Do a search as I've listed all of the details for the chain and the two sprocket suppliers there. The chain is a DID ZVM-X and is currently the strongest 520 on the market. All up price was prety reasonable:

$129 - Chain
$54 - Rear sprocket
$25 - Front Sprocket

The new axel blocks don't really help at all. There is no reference mark on the swingarm worth trying to use for alignment. I adjust my wheel alignment by measuring the distance from front of axel block to surface the adjustment bolts are threaded into. This surface was controlled during the machining process of the swingarm using the axle bore as a datum. You can verify it's the same from side to side by measuring from flat surface to the swing arm pivot point.

Lowrance

2011-06-18 23:42:00 UTC

The cush drive is Carrozzeria's own design so OEM sprockets will not work. They use Kawasaki sprockets (ZX10 for 525 and ZX6 for 520). Better option really as you have unlimited sources for rear sprockets.

The rear rotor is Galfer and will not fit the standard wheel. The front rotors in these pics are Carrozzeria's. They have exceptional power and feel with the Galfer HH pads but the rust after just a couople of rides was unacceptable. Got the Galfer front rotors mounted up and they look exactly like the rear for overall shape....they will fit the standard wheels.
