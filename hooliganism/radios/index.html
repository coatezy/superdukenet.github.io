2011-01-17 18:01:00 UTC

My best friend and I have Scala Rider Q2's. They work well. The latest version (Q4 I believe) looks to be 10 times better. But with Q2's we feel like old wives gabbing for hours while we ride. The bluetooth option is nice but I found it to be a PITA trying to connect to the phone and/or GPS everytime I got on the bike. The FM radio on the Q2 sucks as does the sound if you plug a music player in. You're better off using decent headphones and cranking up the Q2 to hear over the music.

Being able to talk to you buddy really makes for a better and safer trip. If one of you sees a hazard he can warn the other. You can talk about anything and everything while you ride instead of trying to regurgitate all the good moments when you stop. Also having the ability to laugh hysterically at your screaming friend because you just nailed a skunk while he was 10 feet off your rear wheel is priceless! The batteries last pretty much all day too.

Bluetooth is not an option, it's the entire technology that the headset runs on, it's the bluetooth that enable you to talk to the other headset not just for your phone or GPS connection.

I have my phone set to auto connect with my sat nav and my sat nav auto connects with my scala, same way as the scala can automatically connect to your buddies headset once initial connection is setup, no problems, I just get on the bike, switch on the sat nav, switch on the intercom and job done.
