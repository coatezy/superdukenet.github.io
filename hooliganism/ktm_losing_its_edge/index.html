KTM losing its edge?

990 WFO

2011-06-24 00:47:00 UTC

So I just read on MNC's website that they did a super naked comparison. The brand new Aprillia Tuono V4R was the center of attention. The Ducati Streetfighter, the Triumph Speed Triple, the MV Brutale, and of course the KTM Super Duke R.

http://www.motorcyclenews.com/MCN/News/ ... on-rivals/

You can watch the video/read the article or you can just jump to what I have to say. The Aprillia won first place (big surprise) but the KTM came in dead last basically because its "starting to show its age". As soon as I read that I thought, well wait until the SD gets the RC8 engine in there! But wait...that probably won't happen until 2013. The competition is nudging that figure right now in 2010/2011. Then I recalled a comparison between the RC8 and other super-sport bikes (GSX-R1000, CBR1000, ZX-10R, etc). Once again, the KTM came in last. Is the RC8 engine in the new SD going to be enough? The 2011 Tuono comes stock with 165 bhp, it'll no doubt have at least 170 by the time the new SD is released. I love my SD to death but are these "hyper V2's" enough to stay competitive, or does KTM need to rethink their street bike motorcycles? Thoughts?

I just got back from California riding the canyons, which I know well from living there in the past, on a Duc 848. Now, it's a smaller motor, but other than that it's the current top handler from Ducati. Even after becoming very used to it I would take the SD anyday, even completely ignoring comfort. The slow turn in of the Duc chassis, the shorter suspension travel, for the street I'd way rather be canyon carving on the SD. On the track, over 100mph, different deal. In thinking about the Streetfighter, despite wider bars it's a lazier geometry and heavier than 848. So even with the power I'd never consider it. The Aprilia? I don't want to be spinning up a 4 on the street, and I don't want to be depending on Aprilia's distribution and parts. The Brutale? Only as a spare bike. Have we all heard the stories about months and months of waiting for parts? For me that leaves the new Speed Trip. The old one had a pathetic frame for hard street riding in twisties, anyone who has tried one and is a good rider with track experience notices that immediately, though the magazines soft pedaled that...until now, when the finally admit how flawed the old frame was now that the Trip has a new one. So, I'll try that bike, for fun.

If the SD is losing its edge then where is the bike that is so much better, unless we define more power as better? The Street Triple is the only one I'd consider, though I have not ridden it yet. For me the SD with Akras and a map is still a great, very exciting bike. Remember, magazines exist not as "not for profit" entities! First priority is profit. And raving about a design last updated in 07, which has been raved by many magazines, is a loser. It's old news, no matter how good it is. Testers raving new, different bikes is exciting to readers and that's job 1. That puts KTM and companies like them in a tough spot. Change just for the sake of change is very important in consumer goods. I learn that hard lesson all the time in my business. New sells. Old sits around.

Does the Superduke have to be the fastest, best handling bike in the known universe, every year?

Not for me.

It's the journo's jobs to split hairs over sublte differences in bikes, and to rave about each new year's models - and complain when bikes stand still for too long, cos they are supporting a commercial industry. Good PR (i.e. journailsm) is all part of the marketing plan, and they need to feed the market to keep existing. Imagine a review that said - "what you have in your garage is already enough". That'll be that day.

Besides, the Superduke is still a class-defining modern streetbike with attitide, a stonking engine and handling, and the chassis parts ooze quality. It's a few years old now, but each model change has brought subtle upgrades, and the newer bikes are pretty much up to the quality levels of all the competition in class. The Latest changes to the SDR are only four years old.

Who cares if the RSV4 Tuono is this year's fashionable bike. It's ugly and has no character. It looks to me like a racebike without a fairing to me. If I wanted one of those I might buy one. But I don't.

I'll keep an eye out for what KTM do next, and if they get it right, I'll upgrade - but I hope they evolve the Superduke along the same lines that made it the cult success that it is today, and I'd be more excited about smoother-fuelling, weight-loss and a slipper clutch as standard, than pointless increases in bhp for useless dyno shoot-outs. The bike is already better than me.

Besides, it's more fun to be the underdog and then out-ride people on those "better" bikes.
...I lapped a guy on a Aprilia Mille at Brands Hatch on an air-cooled 90bhp Ducati with twin shocks last weekend. His scrunched-up copy of Bike mag's rave Aprilia reviews won't help him lap any faster.
