2006-09-07 07:48:00 UTC

I never tried the Touno as it doesn't really appeal to me in the same way. Having said that had a RSV-R a few years ago and it was a great bike.

When I test rode the SD I was unsure if it really was as great as all you read about it. Felt a little flat at first - I was coming from a ZX10r so to be expected I guess. What I didn't know at the time is when the SD is delivered it is restricted until it's run in and even then has a restriction in 1st 2nd gear.

Now I've got one, run it in, had it serviced - this re-maps the bike to get rid of the restriction - it's brilliant. Riding with friends who are still on ZX10's etc, on roads that are sub 100mph that are anything but perfectly smooth they struggle to keep up.

For me the SD was the one because it's so different, really well made and is the hooligan.

You have to choose but if you go for the SD you won't be sorry.
