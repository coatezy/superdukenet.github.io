(Threads should never be deleted cos they are not positive IMO.)

But...

The debate does seem to be getting a bit out of synch with most people's actual experience. I have no real complaint about teh Duke's fuelling, and what snatchiness there is seems to be down to the engine configuration more than EFI, and to me is more than acceptable for 99% of my riding (consider that I commute through Central London every day).

Before my 07 Duke I had a KTM 950 SM - with carbs.
As a big V-twin it was pretty lumpy at the bottom end, and required a fair amount of clutch slip for smooth running below 4000 rpm.

When I was buying, I did try the SM back to back with a 2006 SD and preferred the slightly smoother carb'd engine, but not by that much, however, when I test rode an 07 Superduke I thought the fuelling was even better than the SM. I rode a stock bike, and one with Akras and PCIII. Both were fine, even in town.

...
KTM LC8s are big lumy V-twins and need some smooth clutching at low revs, but overall it's the best engine of any bike I've owned (19 at the last count). If I wanted a super-smooth (bland) power delivery I'd buy a Honda in-line 4.

The only small "issue" I suffered with EFI was getting low idle speed (unadjustable) and stalling in town as a result. This was cured by riding a bit harder, which the SD seems to thrive on and inexplicably upped the idle speed. ...however, I do wish you could dial up the idle speed manually.
