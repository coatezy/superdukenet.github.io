Superdan: I had a troublesome screw as well; I used a set of needle nosed vice-grip pliers to grab the head of the screw and then worked it out a bit at a time using a tighten/loosen/tighten/loosen motion, sort of the reverse of tapping a thread.

MH is right: take your time and use your threadlock; cruch the screw ends too if you can. I'm getting a new motor in my Nissan Xterra right now because the service department left a bolt loose in the intake plenum; the bolt came loose, lodged in a valve and lunched the valve and piston, all avoidable for a few moments of careful attention.
