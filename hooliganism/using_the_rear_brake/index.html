darko

2012-01-16 16:12:00 UTC

I use it all the time. Even if it only provides at extra 5% of stopping power in tricky situations, that's still 5% I wouldn't have had at my disposal.

It's also worth noting that if you apply the rear brake a split second before the front, you're settling the chassis and making it easier to scrub speed when you do apply the front brakes. To add to that, if you're ever in a decreasing radius corner or find yourself running wide, careful application of the rear brake will squeeze the front end back into the turn to tighten your line up.

The trouble is that some folks just aren't used to using the rear brake, so in situations like above you're more likely to wad your bike up
