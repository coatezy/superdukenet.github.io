Stupid Luke

2009-03-29 19:05:00 UTC

I had a test ride on a 690 SMC when they first came out. Really, really, wanted to like it but didnt.

Nice and light but a wheezy underpowered thing compared to the SD.

The handling of the SMC was disappointing too. It was so soft and floppy on tarmac. I had long forgotten just how much flex you get from spoked wheels. Although I believe you can get a prestige model with cast wheels.

It was not at all what I expected. I have seen what the supermoto guys can do with these but for a road riding style and hard lean angles the thing was just flexing in all the wrong places.

I was going back for a ride on the Duke 3 which I suspect would have been more suitable for road riding. It has shorter, firmer suspension and cast wheels for a start.

Never got around to it though as it would still have had that wheezy engine.

Try before you buy as it may be what you are looking for. In London however, I cant see it doing anything better than a LC4 640 or Duke 2 that you could get for a couple of grand.

If it were my money I would look around for an old Honda Dominator or similar twin. Bit heavier but just as easy to manoeuvre in town. These would have the added benefits of better road presence and perhaps a better choice of commuting tyres. Less theft attractive too.
