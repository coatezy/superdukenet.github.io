Ummm hello.... I dont think you all are giving the Tuono a fair shake.


No doubt I prefer the SD but some of the stuff claimed in this thred are funny... The Tuono will walk away from the SD on really fast sweeping corners and streights.

In the US parts are easy to come by for the Tuono, and near impossible and more expensive for the SD.

Service cost is very similer, but compare 1st service on the Tuono and the SD the Tuono is MUCH cheeper as no valve chk/adj needed at 600 miles

Warranty on the Tuono is longer.

Build quality on the Tuono is second to none. Dont get me wrong the SD is very good as well, but to say it is better than the Aprilia is wrong.

The Super Duke is not faster than the Tuono, it is lighter, it is more fun to ride, but it is most definitly not faster nor does it handle high speed corners better.


The bottom line between these 2 bikes IMHO?


If you do alot of track days or like to go really fast 100+ everywhere the Tuono is the better choice, plus it is just complete eye candy.

If you are commuting, riding really twisty bits, or just raising hell around the country side, there is not better tool on earth than the SD

I had a Brutale 910. It was VERY fast and remarkably torquey for an I4. I really miss it......but I kept having all sorts of little issues that turned me off of Cagiva products. Weird electrical gremlins, the headlight lens falling off and then Cagiva taking over 2 months to send a new one, and so on. At the time I worked at a Duc/KTM/MV shop, and nearly every MV came back with some issue. The F41000's would melt the cheap plastic radiator fans. We'd replace them with Muzzy aluminium units because Cagiva wasn't about to change the design. The Brutales were known for an odd ECU issue that I never fully understood.
I traded my Brutale in for the SD. It isn't as fast, but its much more comfortable, easier to ride, and it has been completely problem free.
