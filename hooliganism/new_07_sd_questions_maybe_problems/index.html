New 07 SD -- questions, maybe problems.

onesiccsupduk

2009-08-20 00:42:00 UTC

Two things. The first thing that has me a tad concerned is that there seems to be a significant amount of vacuum build-up in the fuel tank. I noticed it at first when I stopped for fuel, as I opened the fuel-door it took a bit of force (didn't just swing right open effortlessly) once it did crack open I heard a wooshing sound of air rushing into the tank. On a separate occasion I noticed that there is enough vacuum in there to actually "suck-in" the tank walls which noticeably expands when the fuel-door is opened.

Is that right? Do I have a vent that isn't functioning properly? As far as I can tell, it isn't causing any fuelling problems or anything. Bike starts and runs fine, has no stalling issues at all.

Secondly, and i'm fairly certain this is a problem (albeit minor) -- It seems that after a night of sitting (completely cooling down to room temp) in the morning when I start it, it weeps some coolant. Not alot, and I only notice the smell on the very first start when dead cold in the morning. Not ever after that throughout the day. For instance, I get to work around 10am, and the bike is off/untouched till 6-7pm. I don't notice it weeping/smelling of coolant while warming up or riding home at any point. Seems only after sitting all night.

As best I can tell, the coolant seems to be being burnt on the front header right at the bend underneath the battery tray/front spoiler. I can see drip marks on the pipe there.

Please forgive, I have no pics at the moment of my writing this, but I might take a short vid of it (as well as the fuel tank thing) soon.

Just curious. I'm new to KTM street-bikes, and my SD only has just under 1k miles. I believe it's done both of these things since the beginning. I do have warranty and all that, but before I go droppin er' off at the shop again, i'd like to know i'm not off my rocker

cjc

2009-08-20 00:58:00 UTC

It sounds like you have a vacuum issue with the tank. When the tank has been raised, sometimes the vacuum/drain lines get pinched when it is lowered. I would raise it and make sure the lines are not pinched and are clear of obstructions (pull them off the elbows at the tank and blow into them).

Not sure about the coolant. Sounds like you have a leak that you should track down and fix. Has the level in the radiator or overflow tank changed?

I'm no mechanic, but I had the tank vent issue. Hope that helps.
