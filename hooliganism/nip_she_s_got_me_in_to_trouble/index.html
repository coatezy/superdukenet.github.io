Bad luck mate on the NIPS. My advice, based on the last three times this has happened to me......is just ignore the letter. Maybe it's because I'm in London where the whole system is understaffed, under resourced and under delivering, but basically they are relying on the honest people to turn themselves in and admit that they were offending. Superdan

2011-09-08 09:15:00 UTC

Hello mate

I'd also recommend getting advice from a specialist solicitor.

I had a similar case of over twice the speed limit (and a wheelie) and the notice to prosecute actually stated that they intended to penalise 12pts and therefore a disqualification.

The solicitor managed to plea bargain. In simple terms, he said we would plead not guilty unless they reduced the speeding charge, which they were surprisingly happy to do. I got 5pts for 'riding without due care and attention' and £250 fine. I wouldn't have known that this could be done without the legal advice.

The solicitor cost £750 but it was worth it.

Good luck!
