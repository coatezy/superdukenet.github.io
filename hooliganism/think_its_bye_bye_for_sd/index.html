2010-05-17 01:33:00 UTC

I haven't ridden the BMW yet, but I did take a brief ride on an RSV4 Factory this last Friday.

- Amazing brakes
- Best handling litre sport bike I've ridden, despite the stock front rebound being a little too fast.
- Love the sound of the motor when it spools up
- Neither comfortable nor especially uncomfortable (for a bike of it's type).

If I were to spend $16k, I'd likely get the Aprilia (The RSV4R) instead of the BMW. It's better looking IMO, handles better (from every review I've read) and I like my local dealership (dealer support is necessary).

That said, I'd reserve final judgment until I rode the S1000RR, but as another said, the extra ~25hp is irrelevant outside of a racetrack.

One interesting note - the Motorcycle Consumer News review of the S1000RR noted that the bike had a consistent tendency to hop sideways under ultra-heavy braking when the rear would lift off the ground. They were concerned the bike has a balance issue.

Fraga

2010-06-04 18:42:00 UTC

Everything you put on a bike that was not made by the manufacturer or that is not part of their parts list will not technically be covered by warranty.

Any manufacturer, not just KTM.
Any manufacturer WILL tell you once you had a PC to your bike you will void the warranty.
Now the dealer might say it's ok, and as long as he doesn't tell the manufacturer (in this case the importer, that officially represents the manufacturer) there is no problem.
But officially, it's not covered.

The dealers want their costumers happy.
They want them to go back for parts and service.
So some or even most will tell you that an aftermarket exhaust and a PC won't void the warranty. Because they want to sell it to you, or at the very least you to be happy to go back there to spend more money.
They know that the likelihood of an exhaust or even PC screwing up the bike is very slim. And even if it does happen, when they apply for warranty they just don't tell the importer that the bike had them installed.

Even some parts sold by the manufacturer will void the warranty.
Reason?
They are parts for racing only.
They are not for road use, and the manufacturer clearly states so.
Besides, racing bikes are not covered by warranty.
But even in these cases most dealers will respect the warranty though.
