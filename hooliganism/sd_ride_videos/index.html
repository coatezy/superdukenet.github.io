kowekiller

2012-02-25 04:34:00 UTC

It's pretty easy to clutchless upshift on the SD. At around 5-6K rpm put a little up pressure on the shifter, then throttle back a little and it will automatically shift up. Don't force it up because it will then rattle nasty. It works easiest out of 3 gear and up, 1 and 2nd I'm usually all over the clutch to keep revs up since it gets jumpy at low rpm.

Jermo

2013-08-12 20:34:00 UTC

I use the reverse shift kit and try to give a little pressure on the peg before rolling of the throttle a bit until it falls in the next gear.
Feels smooth without much noise either. Bought the reverse shift kit because I've had some false neutrals when shifting normally in a rush or when I'm just lazy. Loved it on track though, especially the long and fast right-hander where the bike feels more stable when shifting.

Need to learn to use the track a bit more, especially for overtaking because I was stuck behind other riders for too long.
