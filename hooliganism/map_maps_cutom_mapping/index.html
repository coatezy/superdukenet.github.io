The low speed running is the biggest gripe I have with the 2008 SDR. It is absolutely absymal. I have balanced the throttlebodies and it made things a lot better. A lot better, but it's still horrible.

The problem, for me anyway, is that I can't pinpoint exactly what's wrong. If it's the ECU, if it's dodgy O2/Lambda sensors, or if it's the mapping. I know that a PC3USB with O2 plugs and dyno time cures this (from reading here on the forum). However, I don't like PC3s for a few reasons. One being that it's an add-on to the OEM ECU. I'm also not pleased with the fact that if KTM sticks on another upgraded map for me, I need to redo the PC3, most likely on Dyno. (With the TuneBoy I could simply re-upload my own map.)

I would simply like to put something on the bike, or remove something off the bike that cures the crap low-speed running.
If money would be no object, I'd install two wide band O2 sensors, a datalogger on the bike and a dynamometer in the garage and buy the TuneBoy kit. Then I'd be self sufficient.

Rant over...
