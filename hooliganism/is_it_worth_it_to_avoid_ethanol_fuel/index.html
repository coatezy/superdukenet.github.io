if i could avoid ethanol i would. the alcohol in it does terrible things to the resins used to form plastic tanks, basically causing them to expand and contract excessively. do a search for bubbling paint on ducati tanks, it has been a huge problem. the same manufacturer makes KTM tanks (acerbis or acrebis or something). the ethanol is not a problem for your motor, only for the tank.

i do not let US gas sit in my tank over winter...the ethanol seems to have a greater impact when allowed to sit unused for too long. if you are burning up the gas, it may be less of an issue.

that being said, i cant avoid it and dont worry about it

Considering the SD has a fuel tank prone to warping due to alcohol, I go out of my way to get non-oxygenated fuels as much as possible. Definitely do not store the bike with any ethanol blend.
Another issue the government glosses over is the fact that alcohol has half the energy density of gasoline, so the higher the percentage, the less mileage you get (I know, most of us don't worry about that to much with this bike!), on my BMW if I run non-oxy fuel I get a good 10% better mileage, and it runs much smoother.
Here in the states you can google "non oxygenated fuel (plug in your city)" and usually find quite a few stations that sell the stuff. It's a huge issue for the power sports industry; personal watercraft, snowmobiles, atv's and other stuff that sits more than it gets used, since the alcohol can settle out and cause all kinds of issues with corrosion and rot.
