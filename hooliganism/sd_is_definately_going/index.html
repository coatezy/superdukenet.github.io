SD IS DEFINATELY going !

russ_fae_fyvie

2009-03-20 14:10:00 UTC

I am seriously thinking about selling the SD after 18 months of, well, mixed feelings about riding the bike.

When its running well and the roads are dry its a great bike, however since I bought in in Sept 2007 I have had a lot of problems.

It was well docmented at the time but the first 6 months I had the bike in the dealers (Drysdales in Perth) more times than I was riding it. Eventually they had to replace the complete wiring loom.

Not long afterwards I had a slight slow speed 'off' when the rear end stepped out on a wet road coming out of a car park, the snatchy throttle was the main culprit.

During last summer it ran fine but then at the start of the winter is was a real pain to keep the bike safe, i.e. the salt attacking all sorts of things.

As I want to use my bike all year this has proved to be a big problem and even the dealer says they don't like salt !

This culminated in the front brake seizing on a bend and I ended up down the road again, this time there was £3500 worth of damage plus the loss of my helmet, broken bone in my hand and lots of damage to my riding gear.

Eventually got the bike back last week all repaired and nice and clean again. However I got to work yesterday and I now had an oil leak that looks likes its from the base of the front cylinder. I will confirm this over the weekend but again it needs sorting. When I phoned the wife to tell her I'd be late home, her first comment was, 'that bikes shite isn't it!'

I need a bike I can rely on and use all year, I had hoped the SD would fit the bill but its really a summer bike (in Scotland anyway!).

I don't believe in this crap that KTM's have 'character', in this day and age bikes can be engineered to work properly and I feel KTM are taking the piss, the days of oil leaks, etc, should be well over.

I have lost all confidence in it and knowing that the front brakes is an ongoing maintenence problem I don't want to end up sliding down the road again.

I have ridden bikes continuously for over 40 years and up until I got the SD I had never come off, I have had the SD 18 months and in that time I've come off twice and I am now worried that the next time could be worse.

If anyone wants to buy an 07 bike with 6k miles on the clock, Akras, heated grips, handguards and touring screen (also all original cans and sceen) drop me a PM. I think it snow running as well as it is possible to run.

I am looking for something more reliable and that can be ridden all year round without worrying that the brakes are about to seize.

Bit sad really cos when its sunny and dry its a great bike, but then again that about sums it up which after spending £9k on buying the bike is just not good enough.
