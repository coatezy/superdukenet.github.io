LED's are comming of age. I have been watching thier use for 20 some years. Things to watch for: They are very directional. That is they don't work very well from the side view. Up to date car manufactures get around this with the use of a reflector. I was wanting to buy a led tail light, installed it on my Bandit and it was not as bright as stock. Reason being it was not making use of the reflector. Another placement for your leds might be on the ends of your license plate. Our plates are smaller than euros. I mounted a license plate turn indicator kit on my ST4S Duck, and asked a worker M8 what he thought about my new turn signels as they were on and we were standing at the back of the bike. His comment: What turn signels? Ya, they might look cool, but .....if some one rear ends you and the court said your binker are crap...

My mate Simmo has integrated flashes in his taillight, and they are pretty invisible. It's just about ok if you're looking out for them, but they don't catch your eye in the way seperate indies do. I'd guess they're better at night too.

They look cool though. Just be religeous about doing lifesavers on right turns and you'll be fine.

As for legality, from all I've read on forums and links to MOT sites, you don't have to have indies to pass an MOT but if you have the switchgear or flashers they must work and conform. I have taken bikes through MOTs without indies, but I had to tape up the switchgear to get the pass.
There has been talk of minimal distances, etc, but I've not seen that verified. At the end of the day, do what you like, but use your loaf if they're hard to see.
