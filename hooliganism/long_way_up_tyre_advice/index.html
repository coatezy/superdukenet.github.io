The California Highway patrol has been using Dunlop Roadsmarts with good results. ( BMW R1200 R-TP ) Grip is good wet and dry and the wear is said to be great. The only bad comment I've heard, is, when fitted on smaller bikes the hard rear tyre is locks-up easily on hard braking. This shouldn't be a problem on a loaded Superduke.

Looked in a 2008 Motorrad magazine tyre test. The Pilot Road 2 2CT wins the sport touring test killing the rest in the wet beating second best by 1.6s

But this was a 2 part test and the Pilot Power 2CT beats the PR by 2.3s on wet track

And also on dry roads and track the PP (obviously) wins over the PR. Actually, out of 15 criterias PR is equal only on 2 points, the PP beats the PR on the rest. So looks like sports tyres is still better than sport touring tyres even for less than ideal conditions

So maybe getting brand new Pilot Power 2CTs before my trip is best (always willing to sacrifice a bit of milage for better grip)
