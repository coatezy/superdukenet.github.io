I have watched many of the videos. Mostly so I don't feel like an idiot whilst Dave is explaining his adjustments to my suspension. He charges $40 to set up and adjust your suspension for the whole day. $60 for the race weekend I believe. He really gets down to it and then checks your tyre wear after each session. I would be lost without him.
I would like to point out that when I first was riding on the track, I didn't know how to explain what was happening with my suspension when asked. As I have gotten faster, I have been able to tell Dave (or Jason on the Trivalley Team) what was going on. They would make small adjustments. Then I would be more confident and pick up a few seconds....new problems.
GoGo squirrels around the track a full 10 seconds faster than me so he has many more issues, which he seems to have sorted.
I guess the point I am trying to make is that I have learned that there are no "ideal" suspension settings. Each person will want different feel and response and a lot depends on how fast you are. I am finally starting to lap at respectable speeds and those tweaks have never been more important.
By the way, Dave "keeps it real"! He races a Triumph!
Be sure to search OTT for all of his videos.

Superdan - Your sag numbers seem just about ideal. Yeah, more comp - and maybe some more rebound too (you didn't state if it just squats, or wallows up and down). Try a click or two at a time. You want to have some squat - especially on pinned-throttle exits or launches. Otherwise, you'll get more spin up than desired (not to mention a greater propensity to wheelie when you may least want it). Good rubber is expensive.

And gents - I'm guessing this is posted 50x elsewhere - you can download the WP "Owner's Manual" from the WP site:

http://www.wpsuspension.com/service/WP_ ... gesamt.pdf

They also have a decent troubleshooting guide:

http://www.wpsuspension.com/service/installation.php
