Hey dude...

Well, the old advice will crop up - i.e. "Go ride them all and see for yourself". Until you do that, opinions are like arseholes - everyone has one - and on here most of us will tell you how great the Superduke is. Natch. 90 SMR - which has a peanut for a tank.

As for the Dorsudo thingy; the 1100 is a giant tank that makes the GS look like a trail bike, and the 750 is underpowered. The Hypermotard gets great reviews but the chassis and engine don't match even the old KTM 950 SM in many opinions and reviews - and to me it's a bit overdesigned... By that just my opinion.

Now go and test ride them all. You'll either buy the 990 SMR cos it's stunning to look at, and you have enough cash (and you won't care about the tank range or touring any more) or you'll get a 950SMR cos they are a bargain and give max thrills for modest cash with acceptable tank range (80 miles?) and they're a bit prettier than the standard 950SM - unless touring stays high up on your priorities in which case you'll get the 990 SMT (esp if you are under 5'9") or a 990 SM with a tall screen, cos it's nicer to look at than the T.

2011-04-12 14:51:00 UTC

You know the list, and the SD still have compelling virtues that keep it very competitive, if not THE choice for many. I have 20 some odd bikes and it pleases me the most of anything I own. If it did not I'd go buy what I felt would be more pleasing. Only its lack of "newness" and power to compete with the MOST powerful keep it from selling more. Everyone wants NEW.

Aprilia Tuono 4: you want a twin, me too, so it's out. To me it's also ugly.

Dorso: I could never go up that much in weight without serious other benefits, which it does not have, and it's not the handler on a pure performance level.

Ducati Streetfigher: More power, a lot, but less comfy ergos (for me, 6'-1"+), how about that exhaust in the heel for bad ergos also? Looks don't do it for me either.

Ducati Hypermotard: It's nice that the first 4 letters spell "hype". What a hyped bike. I was expecting miracles, but when I rode it I found an old fashioned air cooled motor feel which starts gasping when the SD is really coming on. Weird riding position (and I am used to the 690SMC and a true motard, the Husky 510) and detatched road feel compared to SD. Hated it.

MV: Talk with people waiting 6 months for parts would put me off even without other consideratins. Other considerations? How about much less comfy (for me) than SD, still harsher too.

Speed Trip: Not a V-twin, but the latest is still a great bike. Too bad about exhaust and the new ugly lights.

SMR: More weight, without a fat increase in power, less of the "razor sharp" handling feel (due to longer softer susepnsion). If you ned the softer feel, might be for you, but I want a true carver for the pavement. It's a personal thing, looks, but I can't see anyone preferring the looks of this to the SD.

The SD is a great package. Great motor if you don't need 130+ horse, great chassis, fun on a blast for 30 minutes or fun for 400 miles. Still has cool looks.

If you need more power the Streetfighter is the first place to look. Has it all performance wise, real company who has parts (and if you think Ducati or KTM has parts problems just check out parts on MV) and is supported by aftermarket such as pipes.'
