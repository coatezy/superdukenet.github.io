Phil @ Bikesure

2009-07-25 22:21:00 UTC

What is the likely temperature? Also, are you using tyre warmers?

I use 2CT's in the UK for winter track days. They are better than race rubber with air temp lower than 15 degrees celcius.

For summer days track based tyres have the advantage. Because of the grooves 2ct's will eventually overheat and get slippy. Definately use Pilot Power 2ct's also not Pilot Road 2's as they overheat really quickly.

My experience is based on fast group Uk tracks. If you are running at novice or inter pace they will be fine all day. Also if your track is a few miles long and has some long straights they will be good throughout.

In this part of the UK we end up riding around 1 mile tracks with predominently right hand turns. So the middle and left side of tyre remain like new when the right side is wrecked.

SDRmaniac

2009-07-25 22:50:00 UTC

On a dry track. Pirelli Supercorsa SC2 or 3 / Metzeler racetec K2 or 3 or the new Michelin Power 1 would be better as the sidewall is more of a slick.

The grooves in a 2CT will always cause more heat buildup. That said, you will be suprised how well they work. They really are the ultimate fit and forget tyre and will cope with just about everything.

If the track is damp though, 2ct's will be the thing to have. Right up to standing water when race wets are the only option.

If your choice is limited to 2ct's or D208rr's I would take the 2ct's without hesitation. Dunlop make rubber boots for schoolchildren and fishermen here in the UK and that is about their limit. I know they did make some good track rubber for small capacity bikes but fast road tyres? Forget it, sporty Dunlops are like owning a racing snail.
