Senate EPW Committee to hold oversight hearing on E15

SDNerd

2013-12-10 22:06:00 UTC

This just in from the AMA on E15:

Senate EPW committee to hold oversight hearing on the future of E15
Please Take Action Today! (http://cqrcengage.com/amacycle/app/writ ... ntId=34117)

The American Motorcyclist Association urges you to tune in to a live webcast of the U.S. Senate Environment and Public Works Committee tomorrow, Wednesday, Dec. 11, 9 a.m., discussing the future of domestic renewable fuels.

The webcast may be viewed by visiting the EPW’s website and clicking on the link to the live webcast (http://www.epw.senate.gov/public/index. ... beefd81e87).

The EPW Committee exercises oversight over the Renewable Fuel Standard, part of the Energy Policy Act of 2005, which sets the minimum requirements for the number of gallons of ethanol that must be blended in the nation’s fuel supply.

In recent years the RFS has required increasingly larger amounts of ethanol to be included in the fuel supply. This has led to the increased use of E15, a mixture of fuel that is potentially harmful to motorcycle and all-terrain vehicles engines, as well as small powered-equipment engines.

On Nov. 29, the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency proposed a rule (http://www.gpo.gov/fdsys/pkg/FR-2013-11 ... -28155.htm) that would modify the blending requirement for 2014. This would slow the introduction of E15 into the retail marketplace.

Send your senators a letter today asking them to support the EPA’s decision to recognize market realities and slow the release of E15, a potentially dangerous fuel that has yet to be proven safe for any of the 22 million motorcycles and ATVs currently in operation. You can send a prewritten message by clicking the “Take Action” button above.

For a complete background on the E15 issue, go to http://www.americanmotorcyclist.com/rig ... ought.aspx.

If you are not yet an AMA member, please join the AMA to help us fight efforts to restrict responsible motorized recreation. More members means more clout against our opponents, and your support will help us fight for your riding rights – on the road, trail, racetrack, and in the halls of government. To join, go to AmericanMotorcyclist.com/membership/join.

Please Follow the AMA on Twitter @AMA_Rights and like us on facebook.

Thank you in advance for sending a letter to your senators. If you receive a response please send a copy to grassroots@ama-cycle.org.
