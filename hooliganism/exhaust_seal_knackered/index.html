Superdan

2010-07-11 18:08:00 UTC

It can be, sometimes its just easier to remove the akras or OEM exhaust and Y piece, take off the exhaust hanger by the clutch cover. Undo the front exhaust header bolts, take off the rear ones and then remove the 10" section and replace the gasket, bit of WD40 and slides straight on. Then re assemble.

Depending on your milage I would replace the 2 graphite gaskets that fit between the header and engine too. They do not cost much, look in your parts manual in the KTM manual for part numbers.

Had similar, and other, concerns before I fitted the 'prototype' - all I can say that after 12-15k miles of all year use, there were no signs of corrosion or 'fusion' - the aluminium gasket appeared to start leaking once it had become brittler and thinner, presumably due to heat, vibration, etc and was a little difficult to get out, particularly compared to the OE item which self-destructs.

For me, the main benefit is the 4-5 times longer life of the alternative.
