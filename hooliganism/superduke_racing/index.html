Stupid Luke

2012-05-11 06:42:00 UTC

No they did it for 2 years as a one make series which was run as support races to BSB. KTM then had RC8's to sell so the ran an RC8 cup for a year after that. This year they are doing a series for 15-18 yr olds on 690's.

They were / are a good way to launch a new bike. I looked into the SD Battle series and it cost about 8000 quid for a bike (which you kept at the end) plus entries and, at each round, a set of control tyres (Contis). I cant remember what the winner got the first year the second year he (Dave Wood) got an RC8 and entry into the RC8 Cup (which he also won) However, entering wasnt winning; there was a really mixed field. Some riders were new to racing and no faster than inter trackday pace others were close to BSB backmarkers at short tracks.

The bikes were supposed to be standard, it has since become clear than many werent but that is racing. Shame that it never continued as I am sure we would have had some major developments to the Superduke by now if they were still racing them.
