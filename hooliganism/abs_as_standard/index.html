Make ABS mandatory? I'm for doing things better, but forcing people to have ABS is not right. Its not as if its free, and in the only situation I've thought I could've benefitted from ABS, the brief chirp from my front tyre is what caught the attention of the car I was avoiding and made it stop. ABS would not have stopped me in time, and I was focusing so intently on avoiding the car that I didn't think to use the horn. I realise the majority of situations are not like mine, and I think ABS would often be an asset. But it should be one you want, not one forced upon you.

I read a write up 12-18 months ago (I guess ABS might have moved on since) where only in the wet did it out perform a bike without it fitted. In the dry it was not even that close to the same bike non ABS. I think the test bike was a Honda but can't actually remember the model now. BMW was the best performing ABS but was still not as effective in the dry as the rider unaided.

Personally I don't like it in the dry in any vehicle. 2 of the closest just avoided accidents I've had were in vehicles fitted with ABS. On both occasions the vehicle failed to stop approaching a roundabout. I admit that perhaps I do brake a bit late in the way I drive but I know 100% I would have easily stopped the same vehicle had it not had ABS active.

On a off road bike it would be lethal!
