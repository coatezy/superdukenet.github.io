matt_black

2011-06-17 21:49:00 UTC

Pros:
More power. Probably ~5hp & ft/lbs.
Less weight. You'll shed 25lbs off your bike from up high where it makes a big difference.'
Much cooler. The stock muffler/cat gets stupid-hot.
Sound. If you've ever heard an SD with the system, it's a no-brainer.

Cons:
Cost. $2k is a lot of scratch for pipes.

Slip-ons are a lot more affordable & will give you extra $ to get your Motobox & several other choice bits for the same money. My $.02

bic_bicknell

2011-06-18 07:21:00 UTC

Exactly as been said already. Full system is only worth it if you can find second hand but it will only add about 5bhp over slip ons. The whole system is a wider diameter so you have to match the silencers with the headers. There is another advantage in that the "Y" piece on the full system is connected with a simple plug fit tube and Akra springs - not the ball joint that is used on all the other systems - which has a tendency to disintegrate the graphite seal and cause air leaks and popping. But if you find a Titanium system then the colour of the titanium is worth every penny!
