3 Comments

1) Pulling long (ie > 1/2 mile) wheelies often blows oil out of the engine into the airbox ............ Guilty ?.

2) Make sure you are checking the level per the owners manual. It's a dry sump engine so the level has to be checked just after the engine has stopped running. You would be amazed how many people just top up the oil when it looks to be too low after the engine has sttod for a while - it then goes out of the overflow and so they top up again thinking the engine had "used" oil.

3) Engines use a little oil when running in from new as the internal surfaces mate together. Oil consumption cannot be realistically quantified with less than 1000 miles on the clock.

Sorry if you already knew all this - but some don't
