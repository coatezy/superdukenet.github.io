Engine/Transmission Oil & Filter Service !

TC's SD

2008-10-24 18:04:00 UTC

My oil and filter changes are done at 2-3000 miles, so it is time to remove and replace our engine oil and filter.These are the tools I use to do this.

(not included in this pic is my windshield wiper metal strip that has a tang bent to catch the leading edge of the filter). you'll see these laters.

19mm socket wrench for the bottom crank case magnetic drain plug.

8mm socket wrench for the tank shroud, oil filter cover, flat trans screen cover and tube shaped screen.

13mm combination end wench, for the oil tank plug. (torque wrench....not shown), you can see it towards the bottom of this article.

5mm Allen, T-handle wrench for the tank shroud and chin guard.

6mm socket/1/4"ratchet or wrench (not shown) for the chin guard.

Funnel and oil drain pan x 2. ( 1 for the crank case plug and one for the RH side whare the tube shaped screen and oil tank plug is located).

Needle nose pliers for pulling the transmission screen, (this is a wee bit tricky, please be gentle when attempting to pull on the screen 'straight out').

As mentioned above the homemade windshield wiper strip with a tiny tang bent on the end to catch the metal lip of the leading edge of the oil filter.

(For the beginners out thare, it is recommended that you follow the instructions in your ktm owners manual, it is good comprehensive information with torque specifications).



Remove the right tank shroud.



Wallah, it is off in a jiffy with the oil fill plug.... just getting warmed up.



Now to the other side, to pull the "magnetic" drain plug from the reservoir or tank as it be.



Here is oil drained. See how clean joe's oil is when changed!



This is the oil filter cover, which has already been removed, (2) 8mm short bolts), you are viewing the filter with the tool I made to remove it.



#1: This is a pic of the filter.

#2: The cover to it with the (2) short 8mm bolts sitting on the cover.

#3+4: The 2 magnetic drain plugs, one from the tank, one from the bottom center of the engine, both magnetic drain plugs must be inspected and cleaned thoroughly.

#5: Clean and inspect the screen too, save any metallic pieces for scientific discovery purposes.

#6: Same as #5 but with the oil tank tube screen. Next pic down.

#7: The plate that covers and seals the screen with (2) 8mm long bolts, (this screen is to catch particles from the transmission and clutch primarily).



Here is the extra PITA, the screen that will require you to remove the chin guard, in order to remove the screen you must first remove the 2 8mm

bolts that hold the fitting that has a large hose on it, this will get oil on the electric components under it, you may want to place a plastic bag over

the electronics or clean with an appropriate cleaner afterwards. Here is the pic of the screen and its location on the LH side of the oil tank.



For all of the perfectionists out thare here is my vibrating torque wrench, that I use when exactness is critical.



After a very close inspection and debris collection, clean the 2 magnetic drain plugs with a suitable cleaner and compressed air.

Install the 2 plugs and tighten to proper specs. Engine Crank Case, 20 Nm, (ktm says w/new seal ring, oil tank plug is 20 Nm.

Install your new oil filter and cover, tighten the 2 short bolts a little at a time until tight, ktm says inspect and grease the o-rings tighten to 6 Nm.

(use a torque wrench if you doubt your expertise). Install flat trans screen marked "top" going up and do the same on your long 8mm bolts.

Do the same as the oil filter cover then tighten to 10 Nm. Clean tube shaped screen and carefully install, tighten to 10 Nm.

Now that she is nearly all back together you can refill the tank and install the filler plug and tighten, (do not pour the full amount of oil in or it will run out on the ground).

pour 2.5 Liters in and start the engine, let idle until 4 bars are showing, shut off the engine and check the level at the sight tube.

Check the engine and oil tank for leakage. Top off the oil level to the desired level, re tighten the oil fill plug. start the engine once again and

let idle for a minute, recheck oil level and adjust level to the desired level. (I use a zip tie after tightening so it can not back out).

reinstall the tank shroud and chin guard then ride, (ktm says to use loctite #243 on the Allen bolts on chin guard.

Recheck the oil level once again with engine off on level ground, bike is level. I am sorry If I missed anything, this is harder than changing it.

(I do not claim to know but if this was a $120 charge to do this you may agree, if done correctly and according to these instructions, it is well worth the cost. for both parties.

I will conclude by saying, if I were to secretly mark all of these items described above, I would be shocked if the dealer would do all of this for the $120.

The thing that sticks out in my head is not to over tighten the small bolts, always tighten things gradually and please use common sense. The End.
