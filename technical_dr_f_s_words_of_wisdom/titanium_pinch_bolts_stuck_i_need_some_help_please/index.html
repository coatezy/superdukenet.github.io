ShadeTheChangingMan

So,

I've got those bloody tasty nut Ti bolts and one side are stuck in place. despite having a good set of allen keys I have managed to open up the internal hex head on the bolts whilst trying to loosen them!!

I guess Ti isn't as tough as I thought.

Anyway, how the fook can I get em out? So far I have tried:

tightening one side to releive pressure on the other to get it out.

spraying with WD40.

Heating it up a bit in the sunshine.

Giving them a whack to loosen the loctite.

Using a slightly larger allen key (imperial instead of metric) in the hope it would fit.



The holes are not completely wrecked yet but are near as dammit. I am up for drilling them out but what kind of bit is going to cope with Titanium bolts?

Could I slip a hacksaw in between the two halves of the fork and just cut the bolt in half? What kind of blade would cut Titanium?

Hi Shade, hope you get it out without damage!

Here some tips which I used with good results over the years. I give you the list from least to most risky.
1-Mac tools have a cream which contains diamont powder and it grips the allen key in the head for a one shot go, use while heating the surrounding material helps.
2-If you don't mind loosing the fastner drill it out but use a left turning drill! in a lot of cases the drilling heats up the bolt and the grip of the drill spins the bolt out! Sounds unlikely but it works.
3-After drilling if not succesfull use an easy out.
4-After this you're on your own!!!

Good luck!

Would like to add that you should use square Easy Outs as the spiral ones will only add the problem as they will spread the hole you drill in the bolt.
Another method for broken studs etc is to arc weld a nut to the stud,the heat and a "fresh hold" will get that stud out.
