I've found the oil service a bit more of a chore than most bikes, but then it's a good excuse to sit in the garage/shed with a beer and radio 5 live in the background. My two tips would be to check the oil screens for rips and clean them up if not replaced, and to stick some kitchen foil over the battery and surrounding electrics when draining the oil tank ( otherwise that area gets covered with oil and then attracts a lot of dirt and grime ).

For valve clearances ..

http://www.ktm950.info/how/Orange%20Gar ... tment.html

http://paochow.com/forum/index.php?PHPS ... topic=45.0

It is information for KTM Adventures, but broadly applicable to the Superduke, and useful if combined with the service manual.

As for fork stuff, never done it on the Superduke, but I really should! I had it done on my last bike by a suspension chap at a track day and the difference was amazing. Although perhaps that was again just down to it being long overdue.
