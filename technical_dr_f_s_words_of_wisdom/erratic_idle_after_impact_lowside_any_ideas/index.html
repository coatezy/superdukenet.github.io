DribbleDuke

2015-09-28 18:08:00 UTC

Hi everybody, I could really do with some advice. Had a little 'off' the other day which resulted in a slide, and the headlight assembly coming off, along with all of the connectors etc.

I got everything fixed up and went for a ride, and found that the revs fluctuate erraticly around 2 to 3.5k, a bit disconcerting when you're approaching a corner relying on engine braking! I also noticed that the engine was running slightly hotter than usual.

So when I got back I cleaned the BGC with brake cleaner, dried with compressed air and coated in WD40 before putting back together. When I restarted the bike the revs went straight to 4k and stayed there.

Today I checked the TPS values (both from cold) and they are front (black connector) = 0.78V and rear (white connector) = 1.05V. I realise the front should be 0.6V but I checked it cold as I didnt want a cold engine running at 4k revs.

Do you think the issue is the front TPS?

I suspect it's something to do with the gubbins in the headlight assembly, or the reg/rec, but don't want to check the reg/rec yet as it involves running the engine.

Any thoughts on the TPS, and what to check next? Thanks for your help...
