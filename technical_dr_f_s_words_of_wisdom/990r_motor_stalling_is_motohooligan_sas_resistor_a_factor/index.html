990R Motor Stalling - Is Motohooligan SAS Resistor a Factor?

Davo-Singapore

2014-11-19 02:09:00 UTC

Here's one for comment. My engine spits, farts and stalls. Usually when I'm in traffic with someone up my bum at the lights. Its been scary. Stealer here has had the bike in for three weeks now and has had it hooked up to the KTM diagnostics without any tangible error being found. The stall happens without rhyme or reason and doesnt seem to care whether the bike is in gear, moving, under throttle, on the stand, under-braking, in the garage or on the road, wet, dry or whatever. Its a c*nt. I've taken off anything non standard and electronic (quick-shifter, lap timer, blah blah) and the problem doesnt go away. Yes, I've had the BGC apart and its clean and dry and packed full of dialectic grease). The FI indicator always blinks just as the stall happens and the bike always re-starts. I've installed a new battery. new pulse coil, new plugs, leads and everything. No change.

The only piece of electronic wizzardry left on the bike right now is the SAS resistor. I realise if a cheap piece of kit to replace and one is on order. I'll plug it and see how it goes but I'm wondering, could this be the problem and is it something we've collectively overlooked when considering the stalling problems that are constantly being reported here...

Just saying.

Cheers, Davo

The sas resistor just generates a fault code like Jmann says, wont stall the bike.
Look at fuel pump or tip over sensor.
Also check connector at the ECU, the pins go rotten also in wet and humid weather.
TPS could be an issue but then it would run funny too like sudden high idle and then stall.
Try to figure out if it is fuel or spark related.
