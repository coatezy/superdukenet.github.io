Hey All, There is a perfectly logical explanation for this phenomena, it lies in the rear tank mount points.

In order to keep the tank from being shaken to pieces from the natural and sexy vibrations coming from the engine, the rear mounts points are "floating".

This poses no issue until the tank is lifted, when the tank strut in the raised position for any length of time at all, the floating interior nuts are repositioned, (in a sneaky sort of way).

what starts out over here ------->O Gets moved or repositioned to over here------> . . . . O, it is the time, the weight and especially that gorilla strut ktm has blessed us with that causes it to happen.

linky from my experience. http://www.superduke.net/forum/viewtopic.php?t=8236 knowledge is power !

PS, I know that shouldered nut that is from a early 90's honda automobile air conditioner compressor belt tensioner is hard to find but there it is.

I've had this problem twice but both times it was minor enough to use the aforementioned "screwdriver/leverage" method to get the bolt started. Imho this has nothing to do with the rear bushes distorting over time when the tank is propped up. It surely must be to do with the overall tank expanding over it's length. (Probably volumetrically as well but it's only the lining up of the hole that matters to everyone).

Maybe tipping the tank back leaves the lower, front parts of the tank empty of fuel so the temperature of the tank material warms up and expands. I've never seen a cross section of one of these plastic tanks but I bet they are pretty thick walled - the way you mould these things will result in pretty thick sections around the edges and corners. We all know how much parts expand with heat so maybe this explains the problem.

Does the early pre-'07 tank have the problem worse than the later tank? No one has mentioned this.
