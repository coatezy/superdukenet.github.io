After reviewing your video I'm pretty sure you have a wet BGC... Not you but your bike.
It's common and easily fixed, you can do a search on the subject on this site and it pops up plenty.
To fix it take side pannels of and lift the tank.
On the right hand side near the coolant overflow tank (in the front of it) you see a big grey plug.
Use a flat bladed screwdriver to push in the tabs and carefully pry the plug appart. it comes of the frame tab too and makes it easier.
Clean the inside out with plenty of contact cleaner and dry properly using compressed air or your missus hair dryer. Take care the fumes don't go up en a ball of flames as contact cleaner goes boom if your using the hair dryer.Let it evaporate first but do dry out to get the moisture out. (don't get caught by the other half too using the hairdryer or it will go boom too on your head... )
Lube the plug up with dielectric grease (best) or petroleum jelly (vaseline) and reassemble.
problems should be gone.
