Welcome to high speed woble. The number one cause is a very light front end from hard acceleration and a slight niggle at the front to cause a furious oscilation of the steering due to what is known as harmonic oscilation. It is the number one reason to install a steering damper. It damps the occilations that cause the insueing freak out at the front. When a bike has a tendencie to go into high speed woble it is ussually the case that you can replicate that behaviour any time you like. Which is never, the way I see it. Some bikes are prone to HSW while others with more relaxed geometry tend to be safer. I the bike has a few miles on it it would be a good idea to check the steering head bearings and suspension but there have been many a bike that HSW is part and parcel of thier nature. I am not one that feels that the SD in the 07 and up iteration is one that neccesaraly need a steering damper, that said many on here would disagree with me. High speed woble is more less a thing of the past as engineering has taken the beast out of many a steed. Best tjing to do is loosen up and dont fight anything the oscilations ussually subside at the exact same tyme that you can feel the wet spot in the groin area. Remember that it shakes so viliantly that many times you need to pump your brakes as it literally shakes the rotors so hard they are pushed back into thier slave cylinders. I had a SV 650 that shook its ugly head on occasion and an RD 350 that seemed to like to say no at any thing past eighty.
On many situations I could predict when one was coming just on the terrain and throttle imput. Nicoli Tesla spent many an experimaent on oscilating matter, You get to experimaent while riding a motorcycle, lucky You.

I love slappy bikes, a dab on the back brake will generally stop a slapper dead, always cover the back brake and be ready when accelerating hard over bumpy surfaces, white lines, cats eyes, i would never chop the throttle just keep it pinned, light touch on the bars, dont try to stop a slapper by gripping, you wont!
With the SD you will need to be firm with the back brake though, its crap!
