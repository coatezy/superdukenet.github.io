The only thing I can think of is to make sure the tabs on the bottom of the door have gone in properly, I usually start at about 45 degrees push them in and then fold it closed.

Definitely best to run an optimate fly lead off it and keep it on charge when you're laying it off for a while, I have no electricity in my garage so just plug mine in via extension lead across the garden a couple of times during the winter to top it back up again.

Had to extend the optimate fly lead so that it went via the moulded channel with the main wire due to the fuse holder being too close to the battery connection, the previous owner had cut a notch out of the battery door so it came over the top but after the wire severed itself twice I extended it to use the proper channel.
