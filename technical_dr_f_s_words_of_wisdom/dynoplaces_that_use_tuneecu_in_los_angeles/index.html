MrZ32, he cannot say it on the forum, it's a potential legal/contractual issue. Any DynoJet operator who will install any un-approved software on their dynojet controlling comp will loose the warranty/support/service contract. this is one of the reasons it is very hard to find a dynoshop that will agree to use TuneECU, unless they have an ancient dyno without any Dynojet support... Most of them would never even connect to internet out of fear of viruses etc, much less installing a free, untested, not guaranteed in any way software on their money-making machine. sadly, unless you find someone who utilises 'some sort of system' and will do it for you, you are stuck with getting a PC3, getting a tune, transfering it to TuneECU & ripping out the PC3... at least this is the story i got from all 4 local dynoshops.
