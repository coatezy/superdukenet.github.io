Comrade: The worrying thing is that the needle is stuck at 6k. In my experience the needle always returns to the stop when the clock has no power because the analogue motor is spring loaded. For the motor to jam it would need plenty of water or grit inside it - doesn't sound good.

fazer_1000

2015-06-22 08:46:00 UTC

Something similar happened to mine a couple of years ago. I opened up the instrument case and found some water damage at the bottom of the circuit board; water that has seeped into the case had no way of draining. One of the power supply rails has corroded, but I managed to solder in some wire to repair the damage. It wasn't too difficult, and the case comes apart easily enough. Don't over tighten the screws on reassembly though - it can cause annoying cracks in the instrument cover. I must take the thing apart myself soon and stick some sealant on the case to improve the water resistance.
