OK, WTF, Dash re-sets on the SD.

matt_black

2010-09-20 04:59:00 UTC

After 2 weeks of new-bike bliss, I may have my first bit of trouble. Today, I had just left work early to head home. Gear up, walk out to my bike, fire her up, every time, and the low fuel light is on. No big deal here; this happens every 2 days.

Ride to the station, fuel up, full tank. Perfect. Turn on the ignition, tach sweep, all seems fine. Push start button, cranks over once....pauses, then cranks again & fires. Problem is, my clock has gone to 12:00 and my tach is telling me the motor is at 6500 rpm.

So I shut her down, then retry. Cranks right over, rpms as normal on the tach, all seems well. The battery is tip top, no worries there. What the fook just happened? Anyone else have anything like this happen?

Not had that but it's remarkably simillar to a Tuono issue.

The tuono fix is to fit a capacitor in the clocks on the board between 2 points, basically the dash re-sets as the power drops out for a fraction, the capacitor keeps the dash from knowing about this.

If it happens another couple of times i'd worrry... but once.... not really
