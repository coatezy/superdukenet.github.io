Help...Hot idling on just one cylinder

robinpeck

2010-06-19 14:58:00 UTC

When cold the bike fires up right away and idles normally, but sometimes, when hot, after chugging slowly around town or just generally operating at the higher end of the heat band, when I abruptly pull up to stop sometimes (not always) there is some sort of knocking sound and (now) the flickering of the red oil light with the beat. A blip of the throttle usually restores normal running. It took me awhile to realise what is happening but I now think that what its doing when this happens, is that its idling on just one cylinder. At least I think that's what is happening.

So why?

Whats the problem? Plugs? I had the "15,000 K. Valve check" awhile ago, around 18,000 K. and the mechanic told me then that I didn't need new plugs, that they were fine. They have been in there for awhile. The bike has approx. 21,000 K on it and this is the second set of plugs.

Bike has a PC III and Akras, and was tuned by a pro on a dyno. I have a radiator cover (Cox) and is relatively warm these days (todays its +31 c.). Any chance I'm overheating? But the fan should take care of that..no?

I noticed the coolant in the overflow container went down..but this was during and after a quick 1200 kilometer burn on last weeekend. I replaced it.

Any ideas about what is happening with this hot single cylinder idling problem? I dimly remember a thread that addressed a similar problem but I can't find it when I search.

Shot in the dark but try replacing the plugs. One of them could have a cracked porcelain sleve. Would be very hard to detect. Another possibillty is one of the plug caps may be working it's way loose. Happened on a buddies SD.

I replace plugs at 10K mile intervals. I had a cracked plug on an R1 I used to have and it was a bitch to troubleshoot. Idled and ran fine up to about 8K rpm where it started running rough (acting rich) so I had the carbs off a 1/2 dozen times playing with jetting. Got frustrated and preped it to sell (replaced plugs) and the thing ran like a champ. Bummer.

I know this is not the exact same symptoms (or bike for that matter) but hopefully justification to swap out those plugs.

Kinda strange your mechanic didn't want to change them for you. It takes some work (removing airbox etc.) but he would have had to do that any way to check the valves. Plugs would've been easy to get to. My point being are you sure he actually got in there and checked your valves?

Just sayin.....

Good luck and let us know what you find.
