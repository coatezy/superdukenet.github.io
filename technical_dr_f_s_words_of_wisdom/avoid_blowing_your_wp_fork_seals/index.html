Avoid blowing your WP fork seals

Spyder

2007-03-13 02:34:00 UTC

Perhaps someone has already posted about this, but I couldn't find anyhing.

In doing some homework about the WP forks I came across an item that can save you from some expensive fork repair later down the line. You may have noticed that there are two screws on the top of each fork leg. Thses are there to release pressure from inside the fork leg. Over time the WP forks build up pressure. This pressure can eventually blow the fork seals. The factory owners manual indicates that you should bleed the fork legs every 1000km.

Rather than bogarting your screws by constantly unscrewing them to release the pressure why not go out and Now with a simple push of a button I can release the pressure with a satisfying 'tsss' every few rides. Cheap piece of mind to release the pressure. After two weeks with my release valves it is interesting to see how much pressure can build up in the fork after one ride!
