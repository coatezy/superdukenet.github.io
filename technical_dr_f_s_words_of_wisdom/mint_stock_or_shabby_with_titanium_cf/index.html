Mint stock...or shabby with titanium/cf

pingvin

2008-05-16 20:13:00 UTC

Yeah, that's my bike. Didn't feel I was going that fast but it was only 5 degrees (that's Celsius here in metric world). So I guess I was going to fast for that temperature...obviously.



So the dealer had a look at it. More or less all plastic is in pieces including the tank. At least one of the fork legs is bent. The front wheel is bent and probably the discs. Bar is bent. Radiator deformed. Everything behind the motorspoiler is crushed including exhaust pipe. But that exhaust pipe might have saved the motor taking up the energy. Frame scratched, swing arm scratched, clutch/engine cover scratched. Indicators, mirrors, bar ends, levers etc scratched, bent or broken. Slip on cans scratched (but not much).

Still the dealer think it might be possible to avoid a write off. Motor is fine, intruments are fine, head light is fine, back wheel is fine. And except a few scratches frame and swingarm seems fine. In the end I think it's up to me. Either they fix it or I get the insurance money. Except there are more options.

One is they got another also crashed 07 SDR, but it's much less damaged, mostly plastic parts. So I can have that one in a couple of days with pretty much exactly the same spec (even the low milage).

Another option is just doing some touch up work on the frame and swing arm saving all the disassembly work and use the money on cf wheels and full exhaust system. And maybe more cf parts if there are any money left. Also I might leave some other scratced parts on as long as they work fine.

So my alternatives are:

1. Get the insurance money and buy the other more or less stock SDR
2. Live with a not perfect finish but have plenty of titanium and cf

I'm having big problems deciding. On one hand I don't mind a few scratches. Bikes are for using. On the other side, the SDR are capable enough stock so does some cf and titanium really make that much difference?

Or take the money and but a 990SM. Or RC8 Well, I've only had the SDR for a few weeks so I think I'll stick to that for now.

I don't see the big point in getting my bike back in stock condition though. After all it's been through a big crash so I'll rather have the other one.
