Also there is the MRA but I have yet to see a picture.

I tried my best to drum up enough support on this forum to get MRA to go into production with their prototype SD Vario Touring screen, but there was so much negative stuff from people who derided the very idea for a screen, I deleted all the posts and gave up - because I was always going to get one anyway. Like the guy that bragged about riding thousands of miles a week at 3 digit speeds, who thought anyone who wanted a screen was a pussy. Obviously the windblast was being deflected by the huge balls sat on top of his fuel tank...

Anyway, I am in possession of the one and only never to be sold MRA prototype screen and it works superbly. The 10 position adjustable aerofoil on the top is great and easily adjustable whilst riding. The whole thing 5 minutes to take off and I for one, can live with the four button head screws left in the headlamp mask. You could however velcro it on, if you were really against drilling into your bike.

All academic really, thanks to the 'neggies' on this forum.

2007-06-02 10:07:00 UTC

I can see the potential benefit too. However, the strange experience I have is that the SD turned to be a much more comfortable touring bike for me than the 950 ADV I had before it.

Last week we did a trip through the Eifel in Germany and did some 1300 KMs of twisty roads in 3 days. For me the riding position on the SD is much better, the seat is MUCH better and the absence or turbulence and the deafening noise from the ADV fairing makes a night & day difference. At the end of an 8 hour ride I climb off the SD much fitter than I did from the ADV who is supposed made for that sort of thing.

Obviously the stress of the windblast highly depends on your clothing too. In thight leathers 150 to 160 KM/H is fine for longer distances.

I am curious if an extended screen will generate turbulence and noise again.
