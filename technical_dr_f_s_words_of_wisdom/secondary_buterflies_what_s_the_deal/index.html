Do NOT remove the secondary butterflies.

If you do, you will experience a momentary lean at WOT settings below what ever rpm the butterflies are partially open. Additionally, you will loose some top end power; the presence of the butterflies in the intake flow creates turbulence which actually enhances intake volume and intake power.

We need to find out how the plates operate: what rpm do they open. These plates are solely performance and noise controllers. A more productive experiement would be to try plates of different smaller diameters and find out which ones deliver better power; it will also give better throttle response. This may be the last area in which mproved performance may be realised from the current LC8 motor.
