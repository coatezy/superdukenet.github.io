Let me tell you something first.

I love my bike. The handling is mindblowing (for a big bike) and the components, suspensions, brakes are all excellent. Even the engine is good, if you keep it high enough in the revs range.

What I don't like is the lack of clearness from KTM.

I just don't believe that the problem with the bike's fueling is unknown to them. Come on guys, we're not talking about some third class bike mechanic here. KTM is the second biggest bike manufacturer in Europe. Their engineers have developped race winning machines in all categories. They're currently planning to run the RC8 (RC8-R ?) in WSB and kick Honda,Yamaha and Ducati's arses.

Yet the same people, working (and testing) for years on the SD/LC8 project and its high-tech FI, somehow failed to notice there was a small flaw in the system? I don't buy it.

So what's the problem? Is it the (ridiculous) Euro3 Emission Regulations? Lack of development of the ECU? Is there something we can do about it?

Eventually I will (like others before me) make this engine run as it should. Official feedback, through dealers or even better through this forum, from KTM would be apreciated.
