fact 5 are you sure its not air/steam escaping from the swingarm through the fittings? The swingarm gets bloody hot and unless you have drilled holes in the underneath at the wheel end to let water escape that gets in it will let steam out the only way it can which is through the mudguard, chain guard, chain guide and brake line brackets.

Facts 1 and 3 - assuming you have the OE crankcase breather set up, open up your airbox and if you find a lot of oil/oil mist, your balancer shaft seal may have gone, as mine did with similar symptoms.

Can a blown oil seal affect oil pressure? Doubt it but if so, this might explain low rev front cylinder cam chain slap (Fact 2) but, as per the other thread, a sticking front cylinder cam chain hydraulic tensioner is, as for me, an early symptom of coolant in the oil due to failed water pump bearing, shaft and seal.
