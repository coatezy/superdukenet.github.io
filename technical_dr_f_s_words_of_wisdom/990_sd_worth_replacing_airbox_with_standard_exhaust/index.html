ktmguy

2014-12-30 10:08:00 UTC

The standard exhaust has the cat in it, to clean it the sas purges air in to it, so you need to retain that.
If you can keep the sas with the rotty filter and sort the fuel map out you probably still find some gains and save fuel as the standard setup is pretty rough.

Otherwise pretty much a waste of time!
The original 990 exhaust is restrictive and weights a ton too, I wouldn't want to drag that around on a track. There must be something out there that is not too loud.

From advrider

It's not necessary to remove the cats if you remove the SAS. I ran for at least 10,000 miles with no SAS and cats still in the standard cans. No ill effects. When I finally cut the cat out, it was not blocked or clogged in any way.

In fact I'd say, to be honest, removing the cat is a waste of time and effort. By all means do it if it makes you happy, but it won't make the exhaust run any cooler, it doesn't change the noise it makes, and it doesnt affect the flow to any noticable degree.

Another one

Just a word or two on cat's and SAS. First of the cats job is to burn unburnt hydrocarbons that leave the combustion camber, this is a good thing. One down side of this is they are hot as a result. Now others have removed them and not seen a significant decrease in the temp of the mufflers, my guess is because of the design of the baffles in the muffler and not the cats. So the benefit of removal in marginal, if not detrimental to the enviroment. Now as far as the SAS goes, its job is to introduce O2(air) to the cat during warm up(of the eng) to aid the cats to get to operating temp faster, so they work at peak eficiancy sooner. It will not damage the cats if the SAS does not funtion, they will still work, they will just take longer to heat. Many vehicles do not use SAS with cats. So if it were me, I'd remove the SAS and leave the cats. And when I can, just get some A/M cans.
