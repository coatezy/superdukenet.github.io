Oil Pressure Light

DGShannon

2012-07-30 18:22:00 UTC

Did a forum search first, and didn't turn up any answers, so here goes...

In the past couple of months, it has only happened to me twice, and always near the end of a long, HOT (96 to 100F) day riding.
When I pull up to a stop, my oil pressure light blinks on and off, pulsing at engine RPM. Blip the throttle, and it goes away.

The first time, it happened, the throttle blip stopped it, and it didn't come back. That was about 6 weeks ago.
Yesterday, it happened again. Blip turned if off, but as RPM dropped, it started again. Second blip stopped it, and it stayed off.

Sort of like the oil hot, and thin, and the oil pressure drops just low enough, to pulse the light, with the RPM.
Power stroke pushes enough pressure, and exhaust stroke does not. On, off, on, off, on, off, etc.

Anyone want to venture an opinion, on this one?

Thanks,
Darrell

Check the wiring close to the pressure switch - on the SDR, at least, it can sit on a coolant hose clamp which rubs thru' the insulation, shorts the wiring and the warning light comes on.
