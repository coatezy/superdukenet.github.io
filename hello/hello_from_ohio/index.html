The first is a charcoal canister that is installed as a emission system to send unspent fuel back for a burn through the air intake box.
There are threads here that will direct it to circular file. Bert?
The second is for a factory alarm and diagnostics
The tank issue is discussed ad infinitum and minus a Motohooligan fix is usually taken care off with brute force and a iron workers bar.
It is fun to watch a new owner travel through the pitfalls that are the SD. Like an indoctrination into a special club. One travels through the triumphs and travails to reach the conclusion that you will need a special club to work on the SD. The charcoal canister is just another indication that your steed was unmolested, hell it may have never seen the front tyre leave tarmac. So please, go ahead and molest away.

Exitman

2013-11-29 19:45:00 UTC

1. Charcoal canister: get rid of it. Plenty of discussions on here on how to.
2. Tank: swells because of the Ethanol in the gas. During the winter, drain the tank and remove it to allow it to completely dry out. It will shrink back a decent amount. When you reinstall it, you still may need to use the Motohooligan tank brackets but for certain, use fuel stabilizer on-going. This will prevent the tank from swelling in the future. If the problem persists, try to contact KTM customer service, directly, to see if they can arrange a replacement tank.
